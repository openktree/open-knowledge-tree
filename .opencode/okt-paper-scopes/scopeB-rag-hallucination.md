# Scope B — RAG, the Retrieval-Unit Debate, Hallucination Mitigation, and Attribution Benchmarks: Situating OKT

## 1. Opening

The Open Knowledge Tree (OKT) decomposes ingested sources into atomic **facts**, induces an emergent **concept graph** from those facts via deduplication and concept linking, and retro-annotates reports by searching its own curated fact pool for each sentence's nearest supported claims. This three-part design — atomic decomposition, emergent ontology, auto-annotation — places OKT at the intersection of four research literatures that have largely evolved in parallel: (a) Retrieval-Augmented Generation and the *retrieval-unit* debate (passage vs. chunk vs. proposition), (b) the *granularity* debate within chunking and finer-grained decomposition, (c) the *hallucination-mitigation* landscape, and (d) *attribution/provenance* benchmarks. This document situates OKT within those four bodies, applying symmetric skepticism to OKT and to every baseline.

## 2. Scope & Boundaries

**In scope:** RAG architectures and the retrieval units they consume (documents, passages, chunks, propositions, late-chunked embeddings); the granularity debate (fixed/semantic/adaptive/proposition/late chunking); the hallucination taxonomy and the families of mitigation (data, training-time, inference-time, post-hoc detection); and attribution/provenance benchmarks (FActScore, ALCE, RARR, AttributionBench, RAGTruth, RAGAS). **Out of scope:** OKT's own architecture and concept-synthesis pipeline (Scope A), and the broader knowledge-graph/ontology literature (Scope C) — though OKT's concept layer isKG-adjacent and overlaps Scope C, we treat KG-RAG only as a retrieval-unit comparator here.

## 3. The RAG Landscape

RAG was introduced by [Lewis et al. (2020)](<fact:d8b7cc7a-6b33-4840-89cd-c02b64f3b80f>) as a "general-purpose fine-tuning recipe" combining parametric and non-parametric memory, with the **passage** as the retrieval unit and **provenance** as a founding goal ([Lewis et al. 2020](<fact:ff94749c-0b2a-49a7-9b14-d10c38c6191f>)). RAG has since been characterized as a proven approach for knowledge-intensive NLP tasks, cited alongside Borgeaud, Izacard, Huang, and Guu ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:ff94749c-0b2a-49a7-9b14-d10c38c6191f>)). Surveys partition the field into Naive, Advanced, and Modular RAG, with retrieval units varying by system: [REALM](<fact:5d67be28-39e8-4656-86d6-092f50bc8719>) retrieves **documents** via a latent knowledge retriever learned during pre-training ([Guu et al. 2020](<fact:347d238e-6d3a-4194-b771-0249ffea5b3b>)); RETRO retrieves **chunks** in a continuous nearest-neighbor stream; [Atlas](<fact:b6c55814-fc92-4d70-86ec-4fb2a8b0f923>) retrieves **passages** with a focus on updatability ([Izacard et al. 2022](<fact:4fd9dadd-b7e0-4e3a-8a4d-3de6bbb1d83d>)); [Self-RAG](<fact:71346ff2-33af-42df-acc4-9587c9a13b52>) retrieves **passages on-demand** and emits reflection tokens to critique them, improving factuality and citation ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:03bed1aa-e350-480a-87d6-950d525fce2c>)); and [FLARE](<fact:4a75ac08-b0e6-4d81-8d84-151844090cdb>) retrieves **documents** actively when the model's predicted sentence contains low-confidence tokens. **Structural gap:** none of these retrieve atomic *propositions/facts* as first-class, persisted, deduplicated objects — propositions in [Dense X Retrieval](<fact:d6576607-ea8b-4bd6-af5e-fc335b9f18bd>) are retrieved targets, not accumulated knowledge. OKT occupies that empty cell.

## 4. Chunking vs. Finer-Grained Decomposition

[DPR (Karpukhin et al. 2020)](<fact:84412fe6-5b77-4c25-bec5-aa03ba457c18>) established the **passage** as the default dense-retrieval unit, with later works confirming dense passage retrieval as the standard for open-domain QA ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:b01cd3fe-51f7-4e20-966e-e1eb3d671a40>)). Standard chunking strategies include fixed-size token splitting, which "risks creating cross-section cuts" ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:598915f9-0839-4390-8c89-0e2d3b77892b>)), semantic cluster chunking via TF-IDF + k-means ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:9a897c65-fc56-4ca5-8f51-e476efecff10>)), and adaptive chunking that extends on cosine similarity > 0.8 with micro-headers ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:454d83ee-3ee0-4c07-b982-ff3282608b21>)). Empirically, on a clinical-decision benchmark, Adaptive chunking reached F1 0.63 vs. Proposition chunking F1 0.49 vs. Basic RAG F1 0.21 ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:de7e0df1-58d1-4c2a-bb48-acfe62f7c659>), [Proposition](<fact:d5673765-a3e9-4dfb-8555-c3ba9d330aa7>), [Basic](<fact:713811a5-3ffa-4bbc-a775-505c66ada998>)) — though on a single domain, with wide CIs.

The critique of chunking has two prongs. **Prong 1 (chunks lose context):** naive chunking embeds short windows without long-document context; [late chunking (Günther et al. 2024)](<fact:b9c9025e-f9cd-4b92-9099-25257cf7c93c>) embeds all tokens of a long text first, then chunks just before mean pooling, "preserving" the chunk as a unit but contextualizing it ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:8f57f62b-6d94-47e8-ad9c-8f4220cbcede>), [per OKT fact pool](<fact:a3e66175-816b-4275-82a5-3f41278b9c29>)). **Prong 2 (chunks are the wrong granularity):** [Dense X Retrieval (Chen et al. 2023)](<fact:d6576607-ea8b-4bd6-af5e-fc335b9f18bd>) argues propositions — "atomic expressions... self-contained" ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:ed206e2d-7069-451e-8f65-4a61bc379404>)) — significantly outperform passage-level units in retrieval. Proposition-based chunking extracts atomic claims per sentence at temperature ≈ 0.2, grouped into ~500-word chunks ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:5856024f-cc14-4997-863c-375244a3b8f2>)).

### Parallel Scenarios on Granularity

**Scenario A — Factual atomicity is the right unit (OKT's wager).** The evidence: [Dense X Retrieval](<fact:d6576607-ea8b-4bd6-af5e-fc335b9f18bd>) shows propositions outperform passages; [FActScore](<fact:4d72c485-8c61-4602-838d-2d974eac7d4a>) decomposes generations into atomic facts to *evaluate* them; proposition chunking raises precision over basic RAG ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:d5673765-a3e9-4dfb-8555-c3ba9d330aa7>)). The convergence: meaning lives at claim-level, not window-level. OKT is the most committed version of this wager — it persists propositions as durable rows indefinitely, not as ephemeral retrieval targets.

**Scenario B — Context coherence must be preserved (the extraction-risk view).** The evidence: chunking strategies that preserve broader context (adaptive, late-chunking) [outperform](<fact:de7e0df1-58d1-4c2a-bb48-acfe62f7c659>) proposition chunking on at least one benchmark; extraction of atomic propositions is itself an LLM call that can drop qualifiers, hedges, or scope; and "rule-based chunking in RAG can itself cause hallucinations" via chunks that "lack necessary semantic information" (per OKT [hallucinations](<concept:e8ce842a-3746-46dd-ac89-e7d724484614>) synthesis, citing [13,14]).

**Where OKT lands:** OKT sides with Scenario A but attempts to recover what B worries about by preserving **sentence-level provenance** — each fact carries back-references (`fact_references`) to its originating sentence — so context can be re-anchored at read time. The bet: atomicity + provenance recovers context that extraction might otherwise flatten. This is subject to extraction-quality risk, which OKT does not eliminate.

## 5. How OKT's Fact-Decomposition Relates

OKT differs from proposition retrieval in four ways. (1) **Persistence:** facts are durable rows in a compounding pool, not ephemeral retrieval targets regenerated per query. (2) **Deduplication:** facts are deduplicated cross-source, with provenance unioned — a fact supported by N sources records all N, unlike chunks which are content-addressed by hash. (3) **Concept linking:** facts induce emergent [concepts](<concept:39221de8-c988-41b8-a616-d06520f32427>) via grouping; concepts acquire [related concepts](<concept:4b7ad817-17c3-4d82-9a9d-cd8ef1f803d1>) by shared-fact count — a derived relation layer absent from proposition retrieval. (4) **Sentence-level provenance:** each fact points back to its source sentence, addressing Scenario B's context-coherence concern at the storage layer. **Honest limitation:** extraction is an LLM call and inherits the same hallucination risk Scenario B identifies — OKT is not immune to extractor error, and [RAGTruth](<concept:0a0359a5-a60b-4b9c-a093-b6eb549f103b>) shows that extraction-LLM hallucination is a documented failure mode within RAG pipelines (see §10).

## 6. The Hallucination-Mitigation Landscape

[The Siren's Song survey (Zhang et al. 2023)](<fact:42e347b5-881e-4364-9556-e391c7cefba1>) and the [comprehensive mitigation survey (Tonmoy et al. 2024)](<fact:76b4e383-532d-496b-b53f-ac7e9d77641e>) organize the field. The Tonmoy survey enumerates "over 32 techniques" ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:b06c79e2-f143-43e3-86a4-97ab63a63bab>)) under a taxonomy spanning dataset utilization, tasks, feedback mechanisms, and retriever types ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:531aa370-3563-4ed6-89d6-4cb9a66c0316>)). Synthesizing across surveys, the families are: **data-related** (cleaning, filtering); **training-time** (RLHF, factuality tuning, [Self-RAG reflection tokens](<fact:677a80ca-0d23-4ea8-99ca-9f258784e236>)); **inference-time** (retrieval as in [RAG](<fact:ff94749c-0b2a-49a7-9b14-d10c38c6191f>) and [Self-RAG](<fact:71346ff2-33af-42df-acc4-9587c9a13b52>), decoding constraints, self-consistency); and **post-hoc detection** (verification, editing, self-checking). The OKT [hallucinations](<concept:e8ce842a-3746-46dd-ac89-e7d724484614>) synthesis distinguishes intrinsic vs. extrinsic hallucinations (per [Maynez et al. 2020](<fact:2e046d5e-cd66-4250-9c51-4db0bab613a6>)) and notes that in RAG settings, even truthful-but-unmentioned information is labeled a hallucination (per OKT fact pool, [fact:cf3f1488-8b86-474a-bf5c-291227cac021]) — a definitional choice that inflates apparent rates.

## 7. Retrieval as Mitigation

RAG is the dominant inference-time mitigation: integrating LLMs with external knowledge "enhances performance on knowledge-based tasks" and mitigates hallucination ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:5320111b-717e-40a3-afa7-061dd1070738>)). [Self-RAG](<fact:03bed1aa-e350-480a-87d6-950d525fce2c>) adds self-reflection tokens, making the model "controllable during inference" ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:677a80ca-0d23-4ea8-99ca-9f258784e236>)) and improving both factuality and citation. The thrust: retrieval helps when evidence is **relevant** and the model **uses it faithfully**. The OKT hallucinations synthesis's parallel-scenario analysis surfaces the tension honestly: in Scenario A, "hallucinations remain a critical, unsolved barrier" — [RAGTruth](<fact:3a087c2e-bde2-41ef-925e-57b986ef3a27>) reports a 43.1% overall rate even with retrieval, and the survey "explicitly states that retrieval-augmented LLMs still face limitations"; in Scenario B, RAG/KG-RAG "demonstrably improve reliability" on benchmarks but with contested measurement. OKT inherits Scenario A's caveat: retrieval helps, but does not by itself solve hallucination.

## 8. Self-Checking and Uncertainty-Based Detection

[SelfCheckGPT (Manakul et al. 2023)](<fact:c267a3e7-0f59-4348-bdef-218c4487c3fa>) is a post-hoc, **black-box, zero-resource** method: it samples multiple responses and measures inter-sample consistency, requiring no external database ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:44a33505-23aa-4792-a9be-168c87cc9951>)). Its black-box-with-prompt variant achieves the highest AUC-PR on WikiBio sentence-level non-factual detection ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:666fd4a2-b359-490f-a1a6-f30166f18ad4>)) and highest Pearson/Spearman at passage level ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:e3ab53e5-3049-457c-88c1-68980866e628>)). [Semantic entropy (Farquhar et al. 2024, Nature)](<fact:a3cdf0ee-c71f-4eea-b8f4-5cad8178e266>) generalizes this: it estimates meaning-variance over samples rather than token-variance, detecting **confabulations** — fluent claims sensitive to irrelevant details like the random seed (per OKT [hallucinations](<concept:e8ce842a-3746-46dd-ac89-e7d724484614>) synthesis). Both methods draw signal from the **model's own sample distribution**; neither requires a curated corpus, but neither can certify a claim as *supported* — only as *low-entropy*.

## 9. Where OKT's Auto-Annotation Fits

OKT's auto-annotation is post-hoc and **grounding-based**: it decomposes a report into sentences, embeds each, and searches its curated fact pool for the nearest supported claims; sentences that fail to find citations are *surfaced as unsupported by absence*. This occupies a different cell from SelfCheckGPT and semantic entropy:

| Method | Signal source | Requires corpus? | Granularity | Prevents generation? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SelfCheckGPT | Model's sample distribution | No | Sentence/passage | No (post-hoc) |
| Semantic entropy | Meaning-variance over samples | No | Sentence/passage | No (post-hoc) |
| OKT auto-annotation | Curated fact pool | Yes (its own compounding pool) | Sentence | No (post-hoc, surfaces by absence) |

**Honest self-limitation:** OKT does NOT prevent hallucination at generation time. It surfaces unsupported prose post-hoc by failing to find citations — a fundamentally different posture from Self-RAG's generation-time reflection or FLARE's active retrieval. A sentence that hallucinates a claim *also in the pool* will be annotated as supported; a sentence that makes a true claim *absent from the pool* will be flagged. The annotation is only as good as the pool's coverage and the extractor's fidelity.

## 10. Attribution and Provenance Benchmarks

[FActScore (Min et al. 2023)](<fact:020f539a-686c-4d04-9f80-c32b8a474ed2>) decomposes a generation into atomic facts and computes the percentage supported by a "reliable knowledge source" (Wikipedia) ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:4d72c485-8c61-4602-838d-2d974eac7d4a>)); ChatGPT scored 58% on biographies ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:051d9674-4809-481f-a558-312dcaac8b57>)); the automated estimator has "less than a 2% error rate" ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:12c6bb06-6c6d-491f-ab91-3afa4ee08c02>)). **Honest finding:** the Factcheck-Bench experiments show "FacTool and FactScore struggle to identify false claims, while an annotation solution based on GPT-4 achieved the best F1 score of 0.63" ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:61ddd158-7b37-4783-80da-868032f1e436>)) — the leading atomic-fact metric is, by its own evaluators' accounting, weak. [ALCE (Gao et al. 2023)](<fact:6087b0e8-d6ae-4ddb-802f-04789a274b8b>) is the first citation benchmark, with metrics across fluency, correctness, and citation quality ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:f3ef6825-dc0f-4f8c-8e6d-3424d6f38a40>)); the OKT synthesis notes that even the best ALCE models "lack complete citation support ~50% of the time" on ELI5. **RARR (Gao et al. 2022)** is post-hoc attribution retrofit + editing that retrieves from the open web and *edits* output — OKT does NOT edit; it surfaces by absence. **AttributionBench (Li et al. 2024)** sets an external ceiling: the OKT hallucinations synthesis notes "automatic attribution evaluation remains challenging" with fine-tuned GPT-3.5 around an ~80% F1 ceiling ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:6c3627b2-22bc-4436-b4cf-33a661ebb286>)) — this bounds any posture classifier OKT might use. [RAGTruth (Niu et al. 2024)](<fact:3a087c2e-bde2-41ef-925e-57b986ef3a27>) is a corpus of ~17,790 RAG responses with word-level annotations and a 43.1% overall hallucination rate ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:3532f9e5-457e-4c24-87f5-adb4a3a72a04>)) — directly relevant to OKT because the extraction LLM can hallucinate facts *into* OKT's pool, a failure mode OKT shares with all RAG pipelines. [RAGAS (Es et al. 2024)](<fact:c1833067-a037-4fa1-ac4b-d9eb11c8f146>) is a reference-free RAG evaluator whose LLM-as-judge circularity the OKT synthesis flags as a limitation. [GraphRAG](<concept:ebfe3a08-c49c-4be8-833a-db67e56f9ab0>) builds an entity knowledge graph and pregenerates community summaries ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:5d1efbd1-2781-44de-abb4-1c32891f9425>)) — a graph-based retrieval-unit alternative, but its units are community summaries, not atomic facts.

## 11. Where OKT Sits

Synthesizing across the four bodies: **On retrieval granularity**, OKT's fact is the most committed atomicity position, with sentence-level provenance as the context-recovery bet. **On hallucination**, OKT is post-hoc and grounding-based; it does NOT prevent at generation, it surfaces by absence — placing it in the same cell as FActScore (decompose-and-check) and RARR (post-hoc retrofit), not Self-RAG (generation-time) or SelfCheckGPT (sample-based). **On attribution**, OKT is closest to FActScore (atomic decomposition + check) and RARR (post-hoc retrofit), but its knowledge source is its own curated, compounding pool rather than the open web. **Central trade-off:** open-web covers everything but is noisy, redundant, and non-compounding; a curated deduped pool is smaller and biased but deduplicated, concept-linked, and compounding. **Honest limits:** (1) no generation-time prevention; (2) the extraction LLM inherits extractor errors, and RAGTruth shows RAG pipelines hallucinate at 43.1% ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:3a087c2e-bde2-41ef-925e-57b986ef3a27>)); (3) any posture classifier is bounded by the AttributionBench ~80% ceiling ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:6c3627b2-22bc-4436-b4cf-33a661ebb286>)); (4) the fact pool is biased toward whatever has been ingested; (5) the dedup threshold is a tuning knob with no neutral calibration.

## 12. Symmetric Skepticism

The claim that chunk-based RAG is sufficient rests on **retrieval-task benchmarks**, not on chunks as a good *persistent* unit — adaptive chunking beats proposition chunking on at least one benchmark ([per OKT fact pool](<fact:de7e0df1-58d1-4c2a-bb48-acfe62f7c659>)), so chunking's dominance is task-relative, not absolute. The claim that proposition retrieval is superior rests on QA-style tasks ([Dense X](<fact:d6576607-ea8b-4bd6-af5e-fc335b9f18bd>)), not on long-horizon knowledge accumulation — propositions are not stored, deduped, or linked. FActScore's "reliable knowledge source" is Wikipedia, itself curated, biased, and gap-ridden — a contested ground truth, not a neutral one. RAGTruth's 43.1% rate uses a conservative annotation scheme that labels truthful-but-unmentioned info as hallucination (per OKT synthesis), which may inflate severity. OKT's wager — that a smaller, purpose-built, deduped, concept-linked pool is a better ground truth for a *specific research corpus* than the open web — is plausible but **empirical, not established**: it depends on ingestion coverage, extraction fidelity, and dedup threshold, none of which OKT has validated against an external gold standard in the evidence reviewed. Each system's claim is internally coherent and externally under-tested; none is entitled to default credibility.

## 13. Significance

The four literatures converge on two intuitions: meaning lives at granularity finer than the document, and trust lives in the connection between a claim and its evidence. RAG discovered that fine-grained passage retrieval helps ([Karpukhin et al. 2020](<fact:84412fe6-5b77-4c25-bec5-aa03ba457c18>)); [Dense X Retrieval](<fact:d6576607-ea8b-4bd6-af5e-fc335b9f18bd>) discovered that propositions help more; [FActScore](<fact:4d72c485-8c61-4602-838d-2d974eac7d4a>) discovered that atomic-fact evaluation is more informative than whole-text scoring; [Self-RAG](<fact:03bed1aa-e350-480a-87d6-950d525fce2c>) discovered that the model should self-critique at generation time. OKT's contribution is the wager that these should be the **same persisted objects**: the atomic fact as retrieval unit + evaluation unit + dedup unit + citation target, compounding over time, with an emergent concept layer linking facts that no single source connected. The combination — atomic persistence + cross-source dedup + emergent concept linking + sentence-level provenance + post-hoc grounding annotation — is not anticipated by any single prior system reviewed here. Whether it outperforms open-web post-hoc attribution (RARR) or generation-time reflection (Self-RAG) on a research-corpus auditability task is an **open empirical question**, not a settled claim; the evidence reviewed supports OKT as a *plausible* candidate solution to AI hallucination and auditability, not *the* solution.
