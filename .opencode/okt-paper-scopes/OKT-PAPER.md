# Emergent Ontology and Semantic Knowledge Graph: A Potential Solution to AI Hallucination and Auditability During Scientific Research

*A system paper with a falsifiable design thesis. The paper presents the Open Knowledge Tree (OKT) as a candidate â€” not proven, not the â€” solution to two interlocking problems of LLM-mediated scientific research: hallucination and the absence of a queryable citation graph that lets a reader audit a generated claim back to its sources and sentences. The paper is structured so that each architectural commitment of OKT is held against the strongest available evidence for and against it, and so that six proposed experiments â€” runnable on existing scaffolding in `scripts/experiments/` â€” can settle the design wager empirically.*

## 1. Abstract

Large language models hallucinate, and their reasoning is unauditable: a generated sentence may be supported by a source, contradicted by one, or fabricated, and the reader typically has no structured way to tell which. Two architectural responses dominate the literature: (i) **retrieval-augmented generation (RAG)**, which grounds generation in retrieved passages at query time and discards them afterward, and (ii) **autonomous end-to-end research agents**, which produce a complete artifact in one pass with ephemeral internal state. This paper presents a third response implemented in the Open Knowledge Tree (OKT): a **compounding knowledge substrate** that separates knowledge building (Phase 1: a seven-stage ingestion pipeline that decomposes sources into atomic facts, deduplicates them by embedding distance, induces an emergent concept graph, and synthesizes per-concept definitions) from research (Phase 2: an agent navigates the persistent graph via sixteen MCP tools, and reports are auto-annotated against the fact pool sentence-by-sentence with a four-label posture classifier). The central design wager is that an **emergent ontology** (concepts arise from facts, with only a curated context vocabulary as the top-down residue) plus a **semantic knowledge graph** (facts as first-class atoms, relations derived from shared facts rather than asserted as typed predicates) is a *potential* solution to hallucination and auditability â€” a claim the paper frames as a falsifiable thesis, not a settled result. The paper situates OKT against the RAG, hallucination-mitigation, attribution-benchmark, knowledge-graph-construction, ontology, entity-linking, GraphRAG-family, hierarchical-summarization, and autonomous-agent literatures with **symmetric skepticism** applied to OKT and to every baseline, articulates OKT's strengths and weaknesses honestly, and proposes six locally-runnable experiments with protocols and parallel-scenario predictions that settle the wager. Honest predictions: OKT should win on fact-level precision (Experiments 1, 3, 6 grounding), be competitive on compounding cost-per-quality (Experiment 5), and lose on cross-cutting theme QA (Experiment 6) and out-of-corpus hallucination detection (Experiment 2). The architecture is a hallucination *surfacer*, not a hallucination *preventer* â€” and the paper says so plainly.

## 2. Introduction

### 2.1 The problem

Two failure modes recur in LLM-mediated scientific research. The first is **hallucination**: the generation of fluent, plausible claims that are not supported by any source. The Siren's Song and Tonmoy surveys organize the mitigation landscape into data-related, training-time, inference-time, and post-hoc detection families ([Zhang et al. 2023, per Scope B](<fact:42e347b5-881e-4364-9556-e391c7cefba1>); [Tonmoy et al. 2024, per Scope B](<fact:76b4e383-532d-496b-b53f-ac7e9d77641e>)), enumerating "over 32 techniques" ([per OKT fact pool, Scope B](<fact:b06c79e2-f143-43e3-86a4-97ab63a63bab>)). The honest reading is that none of them *solves* hallucination: RAGTruth reports a 43.1% overall hallucination rate even with retrieval ([Niu et al. 2024, per Scope B](<fact:3a087c2e-bde2-41ef-925e-57b986ef3a27>); [per OKT fact pool, Scope B](<fact:3532f9e5-457e-4c24-87f5-adb4a3a72a04>)), and the survey "explicitly states that retrieval-augmented LLMs still face limitations" (Scope B Â§7). Maynez et al. 2020 distinguish intrinsic vs. extrinsic hallucinations ([per Scope B](<fact:2e046d5e-cd66-4250-9c51-4db0bab613a6>)), and the OKT synthesis of hallucinations notes that in RAG settings even truthful-but-unmentioned information is labeled a hallucination ([per OKT fact pool, Scope B](<fact:cf3f1488-8b86-474a-bf5c-291227cac021>)) â€” a definitional choice that inflates apparent rates and complicates comparison.

The second failure mode is **non-auditability**: even when a generated claim *is* supported, the reader typically cannot trace it to a specific sentence in a specific source without re-doing the retrieval. FActScore decomposes a generation into atomic facts and checks each against a "reliable knowledge source" (Wikipedia) ([Min et al. 2023, per Scope B](<fact:020f539a-686c-4d04-9f80-c32b8a474ed2>); [per OKT fact pool, Scope B](<fact:4d72c485-8c61-4602-838d-2d974eac7d4a>)), with ChatGPT scoring 58% on biographies ([per OKT fact pool, Scope B](<fact:051d9674-4809-481f-a558-312dcaac8b57>)); but the Factcheck-Bench experiments show "FacTool and FactScore struggle to identify false claims, while an annotation solution based on GPT-4 achieved the best F1 score of 0.63" ([per OKT fact pool, Scope B](<fact:61ddd158-7b37-4783-80da-868032f1e436>)) â€” the leading atomic-fact metric is, by its own evaluators' accounting, weak. ALCE finds that even the best citation models "lack complete citation support ~50% of the time" on ELI5 ([Scope B Â§10](<fact:6087b0e8-d6ae-4ddb-802f-04789a274b8b>)). AttributionBench sets an external ceiling: "automatic attribution evaluation remains challenging" with fine-tuned GPT-3.5 around an ~80% F1 ceiling ([per OKT fact pool, Scope B](<fact:6c3627b2-22bc-4436-b4cf-33a661ebb286>)) â€” bounding any automated posture classifier OKT might use.

### 2.2 The thesis

OKT's wager is that a **compounding, fact-grounded knowledge substrate** â€” built once from a research corpus and reused across many research sessions â€” addresses both failure modes more effectively than either one-shot RAG (which discards its retrieval) or end-to-end agents (whose internal state is not queryable). The substrate is built by decomposing sources into atomic facts, deduplicating them with provenance preserved, inducing an emergent concept graph, and synthesizing per-concept definitions; research proceeds by navigating that graph at multiple information levels (facts â†’ concepts â†’ summaries â†’ synthesis), and reports are auto-annotated against the pool so that every sentence carries its supporting facts or is visibly uncited. The architecture does not *prevent* hallucination at generation time; it *surfaces* it post-hoc by making the citation graph a first-class, queryable artifact (Scope A Â§13). The thesis is that this is *a potential solution* â€” plausible on architectural grounds, supported by convergent intuitions across four literatures (Scope B Â§13), but **empirical, not established**. The six experiments in Â§8 settle it.

### 2.3 What kind of paper this is

This is a **system paper with a falsifiable design thesis**. It describes an implemented system (every architectural claim is cited to a code path: `file.go:line`, migration:line, or config:line per Scopes A and E), situates it in the comparative literature with symmetric skepticism, and proposes a concrete experimental programme whose protocols name actual MCP tools, endpoints, and config values. No empirical claim is made about OKT's *current measured* performance against any baseline; the predictions in Â§8 are labeled as predictions. The contribution is the architectural assembly plus the falsifiable claim that it is a candidate solution to the two named problems.

## 3. Background and Related Work

### 3.1 RAG and the retrieval-unit debate

RAG was introduced as a "general-purpose fine-tuning recipe" combining parametric and non-parametric memory, with the **passage** as the retrieval unit and **provenance** as a founding goal ([Lewis et al. 2020, per Scope B](<fact:ff94749c-0b2a-49a7-9b14-d10c38c6191f>)). The retrieval-unit debate has since produced a spectrum: REALM retrieves **documents** ([Guu et al. 2020, per Scope B](<fact:347d238e-6d3a-4194-b771-0249ffea5b3b>)), RETRO retrieves **chunks**, Atlas retrieves **passages** ([Izacard et al. 2022, per Scope B](<fact:4fd9dadd-b7e0-4e3a-8a4d-3de6bbb1d83d>)), Self-RAG retrieves **passages on-demand** with reflection tokens ([per Scope B](<fact:71346ff2-33af-42df-acc4-9587c9a13b52>)), and FLARE retrieves **documents** actively on low-confidence tokens ([per Scope B](<fact:4a75ac08-b0e6-4d81-8d84-151844090cdb>)). **Dense X Retrieval** (Chen et al. 2023) argues that **propositions** â€” "atomic expressions... self-contained" ([per Scope B](<fact:ed206e2d-7069-451e-8f65-4a61bc379404>)) â€” outperform passages, with proposition chunking extracting atomic claims per sentence at temperature â‰ˆ 0.2 ([per Scope B](<fact:5856024f-cc14-4997-863c-375244a3b8f2>)). Empirically, on one clinical benchmark Adaptive chunking reached F1 0.63 vs. Proposition 0.49 vs. Basic RAG 0.21 ([per Scope B](<fact:de7e0df1-58d1-4c2a-bb48-acfe62f7c659>)) â€” a single-domain result with wide CIs. **Late chunking** (GÃ¼nther et al. 2024) embeds all tokens first then chunks before pooling, preserving the chunk as a unit while contextualizing it ([per Scope B](<fact:b9c9025e-f9cd-4b92-9099-25257cf7c93c>)).

**Structural gap (Scope B Â§3):** none of these retrieve atomic propositions/facts as *first-class, persisted, deduplicated objects* â€” propositions in Dense X are retrieval targets, not accumulated knowledge. OKT occupies that empty cell: the fact is the retrieval unit, the evaluation unit, the dedup unit, and the citation target, simultaneously and persistently.

### 3.2 Hallucination mitigation

The families are: data-related (cleaning, filtering); training-time (RLHF, factuality tuning, [Self-RAG reflection tokens](<fact:677a80ca-0d23-4ea8-99ca-9f258784e236>)); inference-time (retrieval, decoding constraints, self-consistency); and post-hoc detection (verification, editing, self-checking) (Scope B Â§6). **SelfCheckGPT** (Manakul et al. 2023) is post-hoc and **black-box, zero-resource**: it samples multiple responses and measures inter-sample consistency, requiring no external database ([per Scope B](<fact:c267a3e7-0f59-4348-bdef-218c4487c3fa>); [per Scope B](<fact:44a33505-23aa-4792-a9be-168c87cc9951>)). **Semantic entropy** (Farquhar et al. 2024, *Nature*) generalizes this to meaning-variance over samples, detecting confabulations â€” fluent claims sensitive to irrelevant details like the random seed (Scope B Â§8). Both draw signal from the model's own sample distribution; neither can certify a claim as *supported* â€” only as *low-entropy*. **Self-RAG** ([per Scope B](<fact:03bed1aa-e350-480a-87d6-950d525fce2c>)) adds generation-time reflection tokens, making the model "controllable during inference" ([per Scope B](<fact:677a80ca-0d23-4ea8-99ca-9f258784e236>)) â€” a fundamentally different posture from post-hoc detection. OKT's auto-annotation is post-hoc and **grounding-based** (Scope B Â§9): it checks a generation against a curated fact pool and surfaces unsupported prose *by absence of annotations*. It does NOT prevent at generation; it surfaces post-hoc.

### 3.3 Attribution and provenance benchmarks

**FActScore** decomposes into atomic facts and checks against Wikipedia ([per Scope B](<fact:4d72c485-8c61-4602-838d-2d974eac7d4a>)); its automated estimator claims "less than a 2% error rate" ([per Scope B](<fact:12c6bb06-6c6d-491f-ab91-3afa4ee08c02>)) but the Factcheck-Bench experiments contradict this optimism with a 0.63 best-F1 finding ([per Scope B](<fact:61ddd158-7b37-4783-80da-868032f1e436>)). **ALCE** ([per Scope B](<fact:6087b0e8-d6ae-4ddb-802f-04789a274b8b>)) is the first citation benchmark with fluency/correctness/citation-quality metrics ([per Scope B](<fact:f3ef68f5-dc0f-4f8c-8e6d-3424d6f38a40>)). **RARR** (Gao et al. 2022) is post-hoc attribution retrofit + editing over the open web â€” OKT does NOT edit; it surfaces by absence. **AttributionBench** places an ~80% F1 ceiling on automatic attribution evaluation ([per Scope B](<fact:6c3627b2-22bc-4436-b4cf-33a661ebb286>)) â€” bounding any posture classifier. **RAGTruth** ([per Scope B](<fact:3a087c2e-bde2-41ef-925e-57b986ef3a27>)) is a corpus of ~17,790 RAG responses with word-level annotations and a 43.1% overall hallucination rate ([per Scope B](<fact:3532f9e5-457e-4c24-87f5-adb4a3a72a04>)) â€” directly relevant because OKT's extraction LLM can hallucinate facts *into the pool itself*, a documented RAG-pipeline failure mode. **RAGAs** (Es et al. 2024) is a reference-free RAG evaluation framework (per Scope D Â§5) and the natural harness for several experiments.

### 3.4 Knowledge-graph construction, ontology, entity disambiguation

The dominant unit is the **(subject, predicate, object) triplet**. **REBEL** (Huguet Cabot & Navigli, EMNLP 2021) frames relation extraction as autoregressive seq2seq on BART-large over 200+ Wikidata relation types ([per Scope C](<fact:1823bd93-c170-4068-8600-b15fbb0b829a>); [per Scope C](<fact:4d7c749c-9019-4fda-8aa9-826d1061c2e2>)), but its synthesis concedes it is "not designed for open information extraction where relation types are not pre-defined" and is "susceptible to exposure bias" ([REBEL concept, Scope C](<concept:5a9fe41f-aa2a-4b0f-881c-ecfc72368e1f>)). The OpenIE family represents the schema-free pole; the LLM-KG-construction survey distinguishes "schema-based paradigms, which emphasize structure, normalization, and consistency; and schema-free paradigms, which highlight flexibility, adaptability, and open discovery" ([per Scope C](<fact:0e30ac79-f15c-484e-a5f1-e6ad06626781>)). **Carta/Giuliani** (2023) exemplifies schema-free iterative zero-shot LLM prompting (Scope C Â§3). **DBpedia** extracts facts from Wikipedia infoboxes ([per Scope C](<fact:f2bb6d17-f5eb-46e2-b4db-9801887f1344>)) but suffers synonym duplication ("length, length-in-km, length-km") and unknown consistency ([per Scope C](<fact:56c86c85-033e-4582-8180-c8b7d8d70778>); [per Scope C](<fact:07b3ed42-dea7-4485-9506-5615c76244f2>)). **Wikidata** uses an item/property model with ~900 properties, P31 ("instance of") used 5.6M times ([per Scope C](<fact:9b426355-00dc-4920-b743-5b952abb86cd>)), with qualifier machinery for ternary relations ([per Scope C](<fact:a20d3785-d262-4272-a454-a8a072ae493b>)). **Cimiano** (2006) is the inflection point: a top-down ontologist who concedes schema should be *learned* from text ([Cimiano concept, Scope C](<concept:b851fb5f-ef36-43c9-a6c3-1480b38cd011>); [per Scope C](<fact:0991df65-0cc2-44ed-a590-ebe3bcbffe7d>)). ## 4. The OKT System

This section synthesizes Scopes A and E around the system's architectural commitments. Every claim is code-grounded; exact thresholds, prompts, and SQL live in the Appendix quick-reference table drawn from Scope E.

### 4.1 Architectural commitments

OKT is a Go 1.22+ service whose source of truth is **PostgreSQL 16**, with **Qdrant** confined to a dumb vector store carrying only `{repository_id, status}` payloads (`backend/configs/config.default.yaml:449-458`, Scope A Â§1). The `facts`, `concepts`, `fact_sources`, `fact_references`, `fact_concepts`, `concept_summaries`, `concept_syntheses` tables and the `concept_relations` materialized view all live in the `okt_repository` schema. SQL is **sqlc**-generated; RBAC is **Casbin**; background work is **River**. Two consequences: the system is **transactional** (every fact write, dedup merge, and synthesis upsert happens inside a Postgres transaction; Qdrant is never a transaction participant â€” its updates are best-effort post-commit), and the **schema is the spec** (migrations encode the invariants, e.g. the `facts` table has no `source_id` column per `0013_facts.up.sql:7-12`; `concept_summaries` has a partial unique index guaranteeing one open slice per concept per `0026:46-48`; `concept_syntheses` is unique on `(repository_id, lower(canonical_name))` per `0028:44-45`).

### 4.2 Fact decomposition instead of chunking â€” Point 1

The decisive departure from RAG is at the first ingestion stage. `SourceDecompositionWorker.Work` (`backend/internal/taskmanager/tasks/source_decomposition.go:122`) does **not** chunk a source into passages to be retrieved verbatim; it runs a two-phase extract-then-persist loop that asks the LLM to emit **atomic, self-contained facts** from each chunk. Chunk size and overlap are config-driven: `chunk_size: 2000`, `chunk_overlap: 200` (`config.default.yaml:356-358`, Scope E Â§1). The model is `google/gemma-4-31b-it` via OpenRouter. The extraction prompt `builtinFactExtractionPrompt` (`builtin_prompts.go:22-169`) enumerates **nine fact types** â€” `claim, account, measurement, formula, quote, procedure, reference, code, perspective` (lines 27-44) â€” each with a description, length hint, and worked example, plus self-containedness rules (lines 65-97), a SKIP list (lines 83-96), and the response shape `[{"text":"...","sentences":[0,1]}]` (lines 167-169).

**Sentence-level provenance via `[Sn]` prefixes.** When the retrieve worker has persisted a sentence array, each chunk is rewritten by `buildLabeledChunkText` (`source_decomposition.go:782-794`) to emit only the global sentences overlapping the chunk's rune range, each prefixed with its global `[Sn]` index. The model echoes those indices; the worker writes them directly to `fact_references` with no chunk-relative translation (lines 362-376). Hallucinated out-of-range indices are silently dropped â€” the fact survives, the bad reference does not (lines 362-365).

**The `facts` table has no `source_id` column.** The N:M link is `fact_sources` with `PRIMARY KEY (fact_id, source_id)` and a per-extraction `chunk_index`; `AddFactSource`'s `ON CONFLICT` clause makes the link idempotent on re-process or dedup merge (Scope A Â§2). This is what allows a fact to be supported by *many* sources and *many* sentences across them. The `status` column is constrained to `('new', 'stable', 'to_delete')` (`0013_facts.up.sql:43-44`). The `promptset_hash` is stamped into every fact and every `fact_references` row (Scope A Â§2) so different extraction philosophies never mix in the same provenance pool.

**Why facts not chunks (Scope A Â§2).** Four properties chunks lack: (1) *deduplicatable* â€” atomic facts at cosine â‰¥ 0.94 collapse onto one survivor, overlapping chunks resist exact collapse; (2) *concept-linkable* â€” a fact maps to one or more concepts, a chunk does not; (3) *citable* â€” a synthesis sentence can cite a fact by UUID, a chunk citation would have to resolve to a substring; (4) *queryable* â€” the `searchFacts` `concepts` intersection finds facts at the intersection of concept groups, a chunk-level search cannot express that structurally. The cost is dependence on extraction LLM quality (Â§7).

**Contrast with chunk-RAG / Dense X / late chunking.** The retrieval-unit debate (Scope B Â§3-4) partitions into passage-RAG (REALM, Atlas), chunk-RAG (RETRO, LlamaIndex), proposition-RAG (Dense X), and late-chunking. **Scenario A (atomicity is right, OKT's wager):** Dense X shows propositions outperform passages; FActScore decomposes into atomic facts to *evaluate*; proposition chunking raises precision over basic RAG ([per Scope B](<fact:d5673765-a3e9-4dfb-8555-c3ba9d330aa7>)). Convergence: meaning lives at claim-level. OKT is the most committed version â€” it persists propositions as durable rows indefinitely, not as ephemeral retrieval targets. **Scenario B (context coherence must be preserved, the extraction-risk view):** adaptive/late-chunking outperforms proposition chunking on at least one benchmark ([per Scope B](<fact:de7e0df1-58d1-4c2a-bb48-acfe62f7c659>)); extraction is an LLM call that can drop qualifiers/hedges/scope; "rule-based chunking in RAG can itself cause hallucinations" via chunks that "lack necessary semantic information" (per OKT hallucinations synthesis, [Scope B Â§4](<concept:e8ce842a-3746-46dd-ac89-e7d724484614>)). **Where OKT lands:** it sides with Scenario A but recovers what B worries about by preserving **sentence-level provenance** â€” each fact carries back-references to its originating sentence â€” so context can be re-anchored at read time. The bet: atomicity + provenance recovers context that extraction might otherwise flatten. This is subject to extraction-quality risk, which OKT does not eliminate (Â§7).

### 4.3 Concept extraction + contexts + aliases as disambiguation â€” Point 2

A concept is a `(repository_id, canonical_name, context)` triple. The `concepts` table carries a case-insensitive composite uniqueness index on exactly those three (`0023_concepts.up.sql:42-43`), so the identity of a concept is `(repository_id, lower(canonical_name), lower(context))`. The same name in different contexts is a *different* concept: `N/Nitrogen` and `N/Neutron` are two rows, not one row with a polymorphic type (Scope A Â§3).

**Contexts are a curated DBpedia-L3 vocabulary.** The allowed context set is per-repo, stored in `repository_contexts` and loaded at `extract_concepts.go:275-285`; an empty list hard-fails (lines 279-281). Vocabulary sizes (Scope E Â§2): **789** raw DBpedia L3 labels (`scripts/experiments/dbpedia_l3.json`), **88** curated "general" categories (`scripts/experiments/manual_select.json`), and ~30 "scientific" contexts per the scientific preset (`config.default.yaml:837-873`). The builtin concept-extraction prompt (`builtin_prompts.go:222-272`) is formatted with the L3 class list and instructs: "The context MUST be one of the labels in the list above, verbatim" (line 261). Batch size and concurrency: `fact_batch_size: 10`, `concurrency: 4` (Scope E Â§2).

**Aliases.** `concept_aliases` (`0023_concepts.up.sql:45-50`) carries alternate surface forms, unique case-insensitively per concept, with a `lower(alias_text)` lookup index backing the text-search match path. Seed aliases are emitted by the extraction model and merged for free via `AddConceptAlias`'s `ON CONFLICT DO NOTHING` (Scope A Â§3).

**The decision is per-fact.** When an alias is shared by multiple concepts (the canonical "N" example), `ResolveAliasMatchForFact` (`backend/internal/concepts/resolve.go:62-94`) picks, for *one specific fact*, the concept whose Qdrant vector is cosine-closest to that fact's vector. Strategy (lines 41-61): `FindConceptsByAlias` â†’ 0 matches is a miss; 1 match returns it; >1 matches invokes `disambiguateByEmbedding` (lines 101-205), which fetches the fact's vector and the candidates' vectors, embeds-in-place any candidate missing a vector (lines 152-176) using input `c.CanonicalName + " " + c.Context` (`embedConceptInPlace`, line 224), and cosine-compares. **Disambiguation is therefore a per-fact decision grounded in the actual semantic content of that fact, not a per-alias vote** â€” the structural feature that makes an ambiguous surface form resolvable in a shared fact pool (Scope A Â§3).

**Contrast with Kolitsas/GENRE/BLINK.** Entity-linking tradition (Scope C Â§6) operates **per mention**, against an **external KB**, with a **predefined candidate set**; Kolitsas et al. 2018 is neural end-to-end EL ([per Scope C](<fact:7c73c73c-f78d-428a-9e00-27022c8c070b>)), GENRE generates entity names autoregressively ([per Scope C](<fact:f5f74c8a-7bf4-4ac9-ae3a-3efa4c46f062>)), and BLINK's cross-encoder "predicts a more specific entity" than its bi-encoder ([per Scope C](<fact:998e5217-4765-44e2-b8f4-6bec84e0295e>)). OKT's `ResolveAliasMatchForFact` is structurally different on all three axes: **per fact** (not per mention), against the **repository's own concept set** (not an external KB), with **cosine tie-break** (not cross-encoder re-rank). **Strength:** two facts in the same sentence that both use "N" can route to different concepts because disambiguation is fact-at-a-time. **Weakness:** no cross-document external linking (concepts are repo-internal), disambiguation quality is bounded by the embedding model, and there is no cross-encoder re-rank to recover specificity the way BLINK does. **(Agent hypothesis, not stated by any source: OKT trades external-KB grounding for per-fact resolution precision; confirming this would require a GERBIL-style benchmark adapted to fact-level rather than mention-level evaluation (Scope C Â§6).)**

### 4.4 Alias merging and pruning â€” Point 3

`RefineConceptsWorker` (`refine_concepts.go:136`) resolves unresolved `concept_candidates` through a **three-stage routing loop** documented in the `Work` doc comment (lines 118-131) and implemented in `refineOneCandidate` (lines 282-530) (Scope A Â§4, Scope E Â§3):

1. **Pre-LLM (all DB, no LLM)** â€” canonical match (step 1, line 307), single-target alias merge (step 2, lines 319-333), per-fact `tryRouteAliasAmbiguous` fork when >1 alias match, seed-alias match (step 3, lines 336-362).
2. **LLM call** â€” only when all pre-LLM routes miss: one `refiner.Refine` call with a 120s context (lines 365-384), gated by a per-canonical advisory lock `TryAdvisoryLockForSynthesis` (lines 393-404) so two goroutines don't race on the same AI canonical.
3. **Post-LLM (all DB, reuses LLM output)** â€” AI canonical match (step 4, line 414), AI alias match (step 5, lines 424-456).
4. **Create** â€” if all miss, `CreateConcept` (line 459) with the AI canonical + AI aliases + seed aliases, then `ReassignFactCandidatesToConcept` and resolve.

The LLM proposes `aliases_to_add` and `aliases_to_prune` in the JSON shape `{"canonical_name":"...","aliases_to_add":[...],"aliases_to_prune":[...]}` (`builtinRefinementPrompt`, `builtin_prompts.go:274-302`). **`mergeCandidateIntoConcept`** (lines 537-604) moves the candidate's facts onto the target via `ReassignFactCandidatesToConcept`, copies seed aliases and AI aliases, adds the candidate's `concept_text` itself as an alias, resets the target's embedding via `ResetConceptEmbedding`, and resolves the candidate. **`applyPruning`** (lines 629-643) deletes each alias in `pruneList` via `DeleteConceptAliasByText`, counting `AliasesPruned` atomically. The re-prune gate is **`prune_threshold: 5`** (`config.default.yaml:638`): an established concept's aliases are re-pruned only when â‰¥ 5 new aliases have accumulated since the last refinement. Concurrency is capped at `max_concurrency: 5` via errgroup `SetLimit` (line 239). Config defaults: `model: "google/gemma-4-31b-it"`, `max_candidates_per_run: 40`, `max_tokens: 400`.

**Fragility (Scope A Â§4).** The LLM canonicalization is gated by a per-canonical advisory lock, but the *quality* of the canonical name is the LLM's judgment call. A bad canonical name pollutes the canonical-name group forever after (`concept_syntheses` is unique on `lower(canonical_name)`, so renaming a canonical after the fact is a schema-level disruption). The refinement worker is also **off by default** (`config.default.yaml:633`: `enabled: false`), so candidate accumulation until an operator enables it is a real failure mode.

### 4.5 Fact deduplication via embedding distance â€” Point 4

`DeduplicateFactsWorker` (`deduplicate_facts.go:118`) runs once the embedding pass completes. Embedding model: `google/gemini-embedding-2` at 3072 dimensions (`config.default.yaml:434-437`); vectors live in the shared `okt_facts` Qdrant collection. **Threshold `0.94` cosine** (`config.default.yaml:459-466`), read at `deduplicate_facts.go:255` and passed to `qdrant.SearchSimilarByID` with `limit=1` (Scope A Â§5, Scope E Â§4).

**Stable-wins.** The loop sorts stable-first, then new, UUID-ascending within each group (lines 189-200). For each `new` fact, it searches Qdrant for the nearest neighbor excluding self, score â‰¥ threshold, limit 1. The `switch hitStatus` (lines 272-333): `stable` â†’ mark the new fact `to_delete`, merge its sources onto the stable survivor (lines 273-289) â€” **stable always wins over new**; `new` â†’ the current fact wins; the hit is marked `to_delete` immediately and `statusByID[hit.ID]` is mutated so the loop skips it later (lines 290-329) â€” the **order-dependent new-vs-new tie-break** (doc comment lines 108-117): the previous "lex-larger UUID loses" rule was deterministic but symmetric and missed same-batch twins; the new rule catches them at the cost of order dependence on the UUID sort; `to_delete` â†’ skip (lines 330-332).

**`mergeSources` preserves all references.** The merge routine (lines 418-451) is the dedup contract: `AddFactSource` per loser source idempotent via `ON CONFLICT` (lines 423-431), the loser's `chunk_index` preserved; `DeleteDuplicateFactReferences` drops the loser's citations that would collide with the winner's existing `(source_id, sentence_index)` rows (lines 432-436); `RelinkFactReferences` moves the remaining rows onto the winner (lines 437-443) â€” **non-overlapping citations from both facts are preserved** (doc lines 412-417); `RelinkFactConcepts` relinks the loser's `fact_concepts` rows (lines 444-449). The guarantee: after a merge, the surviving fact carries the union of both facts' sources, the union of non-colliding sentence references, and the union of concept links. The whole pass runs in one transaction (lines 149-153) holding `pg_advisory_xact_lock(hashtext(repository_id))` (line 162), so two enqueues for the same repo serialize; two repos in different databases dedup in parallel. After dedup, surviving `new` facts promote to `stable` via `MarkFactsStableByRepo` (line 339); their Qdrant payloads flip via `UpdateFactStatusPayload` (lines 361-365). `fact_catchup: 168h` reaps stuck `to_delete`/`new` facts beyond 7 days.

**The `0.94` threshold is a tuning knob, not a law** (Scope A Â§5). The `dedup-threshold-sweep` experiment (`scripts/experiments/dedup-threshold-sweep/`) replays the worker's nearest-neighbor search across thresholds 0.80-0.98. The right value is corpus-dependent; a single global threshold cannot be optimal across both a dense scientific corpus (where paraphrases are near-identical) and a heterogeneous web corpus (where the same fact surfaces in very different phrasings). Existing report: `reports/dedup_sweep_shadow_fleet.html` (139 sources, 4,427 embedded text-stable facts, `gemini-embedding-2`, 12.5s sweep) â€” Scope E Â§12.

**Contrast with Barlaug & Gulla.** The 2021 ACM TKDD survey ([per Scope C](<fact:6b717391-7738-4e60-972e-bbf86d4de6ae>)) frames entity matching as five steps â€” pre-processing, schema matching, blocking, record-pair comparison, classification â€” and flags that no neural method provides end-to-end integration ([per Scope C](<fact:c6975aea-6972-43a0-9050-a48cb9f52b66>)). OKT's `deduplicate_facts` is a **single-linkage** scheme: nearest-neighbor-at-threshold, stable-wins, with `mergeSources` relinking sources/refs/concepts onto the winner. **Cost:** a false merge (threshold too low) unions citations from distinct facts, producing a fact row whose source list over-claims. **Benefit:** correct merges accumulate provenance, and the accumulation *compounds*. **Symmetric skepticism:** OKT's tie-break is deterministic but **order-dependent**, and order-dependence is a known weakness flagged by EM surveys; OKT accepts it deliberately to catch same-batch duplicates that a symmetric scheme would miss. Whether this trade is net-positive is not tested in the pool (Scope C Â§7).

### 4.6 Hierarchical summarization + synthesis â€” Point 5

Above the fact pool sit two layers: per-concept **summary slices** and per-canonical-name-group **syntheses**. Together: facts â†’ concepts â†’ summaries â†’ synthesis (Scope A Â§6).

**Summarization.** `SummarizeConceptsWorker` (`summarize_concepts.go:124`) runs in **batch-only mode** once a concept has at least one complete (frozen) summary slice: only emit complete `batch_size: 20` slices, never an open remainder, and skip the pass when fewer than 20 new facts arrived (`summarize_concepts.go:280-303`). While no complete slice exists yet, the worker emits one open remainder (`is_complete=FALSE`) as the incremental accumulator (lines 354-374); once a complete slice is written, the loop breaks so leftover facts stay uncovered until a full batch gathers. **Related concepts block.** `loadRelatedConcepts` (lines 584-670) loads the top `max_related_concepts: 10` related concepts by `shared_fact_count` from the `concept_relations` matview via `ListConceptRelationsByConceptName` (lines 599-604). The synthesis LLM therefore reasons not only about a concept's own facts but about its three strongest neighbor syntheses â€” a structural amplification step that lets one concept's prior synthesis inform another's. Per-context breakdown is capped at `maxRelatedContexts = 5`.

**Image picker.** Image candidates loaded via `ListGroupImageFacts` capped at `max_image_candidates: 50`; when candidates exceed `max_images: 10`, the picker LLM call runs (`PickImages`, lines 341-356); otherwise all pass through. Hallucinated ids are filtered by `resolvePickedImages` (lines 473-496). After the synthesis call, `embeddedImageIDRe` (`synthesize_concept.go:528`) extracts `![alt](<fact:uuid>)` citations; the worker stores them on `concept_syntheses.embedded_image_ids` (line 424) so reads don't have to parse markdown.

**The synthesis prompt is the heart of the system's epistemic stance.** `builtinSynthesisSystemPrompt` (`builtin_prompts.go:304-505`) mandates **attribution-grounded tone** (Principle 1, lines 312-321), **radical source neutrality** (Principle 2, lines 323-330), and a **MANDATORY parallel-scenario structure** for contested claims (lines 383-404): both Scenario A (the claim is genuine) and Scenario B (the claim is artifact) must be built at full strength as parallel analyses, and "Only collapse if conclusive" (line 401). Anti-asymmetry rules (lines 405-421) explicitly forbid one-directional falsifiability and confident-register asymmetry between mainstream and alternative readings. **This is the only place in the stack where OKT's epistemic commitments are *prompt-encoded* rather than *schema-encoded* â€” which makes them the most fragile commitment of all** (Scope A Â§6, Â§14).

**Contrast with HiBERT/GraphRAG.** HiBERT ([per Scope C](<fact:fb7b2eae-dde1-45a6-b6b3-46e23a759001>)) is a pre-trained hierarchical transformer for extractive document summarization; OKT's hierarchy is **LLM-call orchestration over a fact pool**, not a model architecture. HiBERT emits an extractive summary; OKT emits a **persistent citable definition** with fact-level links. RAPTOR ([per Scope C](<fact:38e8cab3-9137-42df-b282-68997f6a1004>)) is closer in spirit â€” recursive embed/cluster/summarize â€” but RAPTOR's tree is rebuilt per corpus and used at inference for retrieval, whereas OKT's syntheses are persisted and compound. GraphRAG's community summaries are cluster-shaped (groups of closely related entities, via community detection); OKT's syntheses are entity-shaped (one per canonical-name group, unique on `(repository_id, lower(canonical_name))`). GraphRAG is optimized for global cross-entity questions; OKT for per-concept questions plus fact-level verification (Scope C Â§8). **(Agent hypothesis, not stated by any source: OKT's hierarchy is best read as *RAPTOR over fact atoms rather than document chunks*, with the additional property that the top tier is a named, queryable, citable definition rather than a retrieval index entry. Confirming this would require adapting RAPTOR's collapsed-tree retrieval evaluation to a fact-pool setting (Scope C Â§9).)**

### 4.7 The emergent concept graph and the `concept_relations` matview â€” Points 12, 13

Concepts **emerge from facts**; there is no preexisting ontology. The extraction worker assigns facts to `(canonical_name, context)` concepts it invents on the fly within the constraint of the context vocabulary; the refinement worker later collapses duplicates. A repo with no facts about `X` has no `X` concept (Scope A Â§7). **Contexts are the only top-down element** (Point 13): the vocabulary of allowed contexts is curated (789 / 88 / ~30), but within a context, canonical names are entirely emergent. This is a deliberate hybrid: a closed context vocabulary gives the system a stable disambiguation axis (the per-fact cosine tie-break would be useless if "Nitrogen" and "Neutron" shared a context), while an open canonical-name space lets the ontology grow with the corpus. The graph is therefore a **projection of the fact pool**: if a fact is deleted, its `fact_concepts` rows go with it, and on the next matview refresh the concept's `shared_fact_count` edges update. The graph cannot drift from the facts â€” it is computed from them. The cost is that the graph also cannot represent *anything the extraction model missed*: a fact that the model failed to extract is invisible to every downstream stage, and a concept the model failed to name is invisible to every downstream navigation (Scope A Â§7).

**The `concept_relations` materialized view** (`0030_drop_concept_slugs.up.sql:64-77`, Scope A Â§8, Scope E Â§6) â€” Point 12:

```sql
CREATE MATERIALIZED VIEW okt_repository.concept_relations AS
SELECT c1.repository_id,
       lower(c1.canonical_name) AS name_a,
       lower(c2.canonical_name) AS name_b,
       COUNT(DISTINCT fc1.fact_id) AS shared_fact_count
FROM okt_repository.fact_concepts fc1
JOIN okt_repository.concepts      c1 ON c1.id = fc1.concept_id
JOIN okt_repository.fact_concepts fc2 ON fc2.fact_id = fc1.fact_id
JOIN okt_repository.concepts      c2 ON c2.id = fc2.concept_id
WHERE c1.repository_id = c2.repository_id
  AND lower(c1.canonical_name) < lower(c2.canonical_name)
GROUP BY c1.repository_id, lower(c1.canonical_name), lower(c2.canonical_name)
WITH DATA;
```

**`shared_fact_count` is the only column beyond the identity triple** â€” the number of facts that link to both concepts. There are **no typed predicates**: the matview is a pure co-occurrence count. The `WHERE lower(c1) < lower(c2)` stores pairs as ordered so each unordered pair appears once per repo and self-pairs are excluded. A unique index `uq_concept_relations_repo_pair` (lines 79-80) backs `REFRESH MATERIALIZED VIEW CONCURRENTLY`, run every 10m by `RefreshConceptRelationsWorker` (`refresh_concept_relations.go:126-174`, the `REFRESH` at line 162-164). `CONCURRENTLY` lets reads proceed while the refresh rebuilds. The job is deduped per-database via River unique-by-args on `DatabaseName`; `extract_concepts` also enqueues a refresh at the end of every batch, best-effort.

**Emerging relations from shared facts vs predefined (Point 12).** The structural departure from triplet KGs: a triplet KG (`subject, predicate, object`) encodes a typed relation as a first-class edge; OKT encodes no predicates at all â€” the edge is "concepts that share facts" weighted by how many. This trades expressive power for verifiability â€” every `shared_fact_count` is reproducible by `SELECT count(DISTINCT fact_id) FROM fact_concepts WHERE concept_id IN (...)`. There is no LLM-generated `predicate` label whose correctness must be argued; there is no ontology maintenance burden. The cost is that semantic relations ("X causes Y", "X contradicts Y") are not expressible at the graph level. They surface instead at the synthesis level, as prose, where the posture classifier (Â§4.10) can label them per (sentence, fact) pair â€” but they are not *queryable* as graph edges (Scope A Â§8).

### 4.8 A novel KG structure (not triplets) â€” Point 11

Taken together, Â§4.2-4.7 describe a knowledge graph that is not a triplet store (Scope A Â§9):

- **Facts are first-class atoms.** A fact is a row with a UUID, text, an embedding, a status, a `promptset_hash`, an optional `image_url` and `fact_kind`. It is not a literal in a triple; it is the unit of epistemic weight.
- **Concepts are many-per-name.** The same canonical name in different contexts is a different concept row. `(N, Nitrogen)` and `(N, Neutron)` coexist without a polymorphic type or a disambiguation prefix.
- **Relations are derived, not asserted.** `concept_relations` is a matview over `fact_concepts`; it has no independence from the fact pool. Adding a fact updates the graph on the next refresh; deleting a fact retracts the edge. The graph is **always consistent with the fact pool** by construction.

The graph is therefore a *projection of the fact pool* rather than a separately authored structure. This is the architectural feature that underwrites OKT's claim to auditability: any graph-level assertion can be reduced in constant hops to the facts that support it and to the sources and sentences that support those facts. There is no separate "graph author" whose judgment must be trusted (Scope A Â§9).

### 4.10 Auto-annotation â€” Point 9

`AnnotateReportWorker` (`annotate_report.go:123`) is the auditability mechanism (Scope A Â§13, Scope E Â§8). When an agent writes a report via `createReport`, the worker chunks the report body into sentences via `decomposition.SegmentSentences` â€” the *same deterministic chunker the source pipeline uses* (`annotate_report.go:236`, comment lines 233-235), so `sentence_index` keys are stable across re-runs. A `min_sentence_runes: 40` filter drops short sentences before embedding. Config: `similarity_threshold: 0.7`, `lexical_similarity_floor: 0.6`, `max_facts_per_sentence: 5` (`config.default.yaml:467-518`).

**Embedding match at 0.7 / code fallback 0.84.** The configured default is `0.7` (config line 479); the code fallback when no per-repo setting exists is `SimilarityThresholdOr(0.84)` at line 159. The per-repo row, when present and `*setting.SimilarityThreshold > 0`, overrides the global (lines 164-166). For each sentence, the worker queries Qdrant for the nearest facts above threshold and persists hits to `report_annotations` (`0031_reports.up.sql:55-63`, `PRIMARY KEY (report_id, sentence_index, fact_id)`, a `score` column holding the cosine Qdrant returned, a nullable `posture` column).

**Hybrid lexical fallback.** For each candidate sentence with at least one numeric token, the worker runs a lexical (tsvector) search over the repo's facts and unions hits with the Qdrant hits (lines 312-429). The numeric token pattern `numericTokenPattern` matches numbers with optional units (kg, kcal, g, mg, ml, mmol, Âµg, ng, lb, in, cm, mm, m, km, h, min, s, ms, kpa, pa). `unitAliases` maps short tokens to long-form equivalents ("kg" â†’ {kilogram, kilograms}) so the fallback bridges spelling variants. `extractNumericTsquery` builds a Postgres tsquery with AND semantics, e.g. "0.9 kg" â†’ `'"0.9" & ( "kg" | "kilogram" | "kilograms" )'`.

**Apples-to-oranges gate 0.6.** `lexical_similarity_floor: 0.6` is the semantic-distance gate. Each lexical hit is re-checked against the sentence embedding via `GetFactVectorsByIDs` and `cosineSimilarity`; hits below the floor are dropped even when the tsvector match was exact, so "0.9 kg weight gain" does NOT surface "0.9 kg CO2 emissions" purely on the numeric token match. Setting the floor to 0.0 disables the gate.

**Posture classifier â€” 4 labels.** When active (`postureClassifier.Configured()`, line 437), each (sentence, candidate fact) pair is sent to the LLM, which labels it one of **supports, contradicts, related, irrelevant** (`builtinPostureSystemPrompt`, `builtin_prompts.go:578-597`, lines 583-586). **Irrelevant pairs are dropped before persistence** (the classifier drops them inside `parseClassifications`; the worker only persists pairs in the classifier output, lines 529-566). Batches run with `batch_size: 8` and `max_concurrent: 4` via a semaphore channel. A batch failure logs and falls back to keep-all for that batch â€” the affected sentences' Qdrant hits are persisted with `posture = NULL` (lines 571-602). A flaky LLM call doesn't fail the whole report.

**The auditability property.** Every sentence in a report is either annotated with the fact(s) that support it (with a cosine score and a posture label) or visibly uncited (no annotation row). Unsupported prose is surfaced *by absence of annotations*, not by a separate "verification pass" that could itself be wrong. A reader can scan `report_annotations` and see exactly which sentences have evidentiary backing and which do not; an agent can re-run synthesis only on the uncited sentences. **This is the property that underwrites OKT's claim to address AI hallucination during scientific research: the system does not *prevent* hallucination, it *surfaces* it, by making the citation graph a first-class, queryable artifact rather than a post-hoc explanation** (Scope A Â§13).

**Contrast with FActScore/RARR/SelfCheckGPT.** OKT's auto-annotation is post-hoc and **grounding-based**: it decomposes a report into sentences, embeds each, and searches its curated fact pool for the nearest supported claims; sentences that fail to find citations are surfaced as unsupported by absence. This occupies a different cell from SelfCheckGPT (model's sample distribution, no corpus) and semantic entropy (meaning-variance over samples, no corpus) â€” see the Scope B Â§9 table:

| Method | Signal source | Requires corpus? | Granularity | Prevents generation? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SelfCheckGPT | Model's sample distribution | No | Sentence/passage | No (post-hoc) |
| Semantic entropy | Meaning-variance over samples | No | Sentence/passage | No (post-hoc) |
| OKT auto-annotation | Curated fact pool | Yes (its own compounding pool) | Sentence | No (post-hoc, surfaces by absence) |

**Honest self-limitation (Scope B Â§9):** OKT does NOT prevent hallucination at generation time. It surfaces unsupported prose post-hoc by failing to find citations â€” a fundamentally different posture from Self-RAG's generation-time reflection or FLARE's active retrieval. A sentence that hallucinates a claim *also in the pool* will be annotated as supported; a sentence that makes a true claim *absent from the pool* will be flagged. The annotation is only as good as the pool's coverage and the extractor's fidelity.

### 4.11 The 2-phase workflow and compounding â€” Point 7

OKT has two phases (Scope A Â§11, Scope E Â§9). **Phase 1** is the **seven-stage ingestion pipeline**:

```
retrieve_source â†’ source_decomposition â†’ embed_facts â†’ deduplicate_facts
  â†’ extract_concepts â†’ {embed_concepts, summarize_concepts â†’ synthesize_concept}
```

plus `refresh_concept_relations` fanning out from `extract_concepts` and `cleanup_facts` chaining after `embed_concepts`. Each stage chains to the next via `river.ClientFromContext[pgx.Tx](ctx).Insert` with a fresh background context. The pipeline is a directed acyclic graph; a single source fans out into hundreds of jobs (one embed + dedup + extract per fact, one summarize + synthesize per concept), so 100 sources can produce tens of thousands of jobs and take an hour or more to drain.

**Phase 2** is research via the MCP tools and reports: an agent navigates the graph (`searchFacts`, `getConcept`, `getRelatedConcepts`), adds new sources (`fetchAndProcessSource`), and writes reports (`createReport`).

**The compounding loop.** `fetchAndProcessSource` (`mcp.go:1718-1816`) is the bridge between Phase 2 and Phase 1. It classifies the URL/DOI (`fetch.ClassifyURL`, line 1777), enqueues a `retrieve_source` job via `EnqueueRetrieveSourceFromHTTP` (line 1783), and â€” critically â€” sets `Process: true` (line 1796). The comment at lines 1787-1795 explains: this chains `retrieve_source` into `source_decomposition` once the fetch lands, so a single MCP call runs the full retrieve â†’ decompose â†’ embed pipeline. **A research agent in Phase 2 can re-enter Phase 1 at any time, and the new facts compound into the existing pool.** Next time the same agent (or another) searches, the new facts are there, deduped against the old, linked to concepts, summarized, synthesized (Scope A Â§11).

**Idempotency is what makes compounding safe.** The chain is idempotent at multiple layers: `AddFactSource`'s `ON CONFLICT` clause makes re-processing a source a no-op for existing links; `CreateFact` is idempotent on content hash; the `fact_concepts` unique index on `(fact_id, concept_id)` plus a `NOT EXISTS (fact_concepts)` filter make duplicate `extract_concepts` work a no-op; `concept_syntheses` is unique on `(repository_id, lower(canonical_name))` so `UpsertSynthesis` is idempotent; `AddReportAnnotation` is `PRIMARY KEY (report_id, sentence_index, fact_id)` so re-annotation overwrites in place (Scope A Â§11, Scope E Â§9).

**Drain protocol.** Both the orchestrator and the research subagent MUST verify the pipeline has drained before synthesizing (`okt.md:159-166`, `research.md:96-118`): (1) poll `getSourceTasks(repository, investigationId, verbose=false, limit=200)`; (2) page through EVERY `next_cursor`, accumulating `pending_count` across pages â€” the summary is **per-page, not global**; (3) only declare drained when the FINAL page has `next_cursor` empty AND `pending_count=0`; (4) verify the terminal stage ran by filtering `kind=synthesize_concept` and confirming those jobs are completed; (5) sleep between polls scaled to source count: 1 source â‰ˆ 5 min, 10 â‰ˆ 10-20 min, 50 â‰ˆ 30-60 min, 100 â‰ˆ 1-2h. NEVER sleep < 5 minutes. Skipping this protocol means synthesizing against partial facts (still `new`, not yet deduped) and missing concepts â€” the resulting synthesis will be subtly wrong and the agent will not know why (Scope A Â§10).

**Contrast with one-shot RAG / AI Scientist (Scope A Â§11, Scope D Â§6).** A one-shot RAG system retrieves chunks at query time and discards them after generation; the next query re-retrieves from the same corpus, with no memory of the last. AI Scientist v1/v2 generates a complete paper in one pass; its intermediate state is not structured as a queryable graph that survives the run. OKT *keeps* every fact, every concept link, every synthesis. The cost is the ingestion pipeline; the benefit is that the second query against the same corpus is answered against a richer, deduplicated, concept-linked pool than the first, and the hundredth against a pool compounded by every prior research session. **(Agent hypothesis, untested, not stated by any source (Scope D Â§6): the three paradigms may be complements at different scales rather than substitutes â€” single query over small corpus â†’ one-shot RAG is sufficient; corpus queried many times â†’ a compounding substrate amortizes its build cost; single end-to-end paper â†’ AI Scientist's tight loop is appropriate; multi-paper programme over a growing corpus â†’ a compounding substrate with an agent on top. This is a scale-contingent complementarity hypothesis, grounded in the observation that one-shot RAG's per-query cost is roughly constant, AI Scientist's per-paper cost is roughly constant ($15), and OKT's per-source cost is decreasing in corpus size (cache hits + batch-only summaries).)**

### 4.12 Agent navigation at multiple information levels â€” Point 6

The agent layer is an opencode deployment with five markdown agents (`.opencode/agent/*.md`) wired to a remote MCP server at `http://localhost:8080/api/v1/mcp` with OAuth scope `mcp` (`opencode.jsonc`). The **orchestrator** (`okt.md`, `mode: primary`, `model: ollama/glm-5.2:cloud`) dispatches four **subagents**: `research`, `investigation`, `synthesizer`, `super-synthesizer` (`okt.md:92-98`). The orchestrator classifies user intent into GATHER / SYNTHESIZE / META-SYNTHESIZE / LOOKUP / MIXED and routes to one of five workflow patterns (A/A+/B/C/D) (Scope A Â§10, Scope E Â§11).

**The 16 MCP tools** (`backend/internal/api/handler/mcp.go`) are the only surface the agents have to the knowledge graph: `getRepositories`, `searchFacts`, `getFact`, `searchConcepts`, `getConcept`, `getConceptSummaries`, `getRelatedConcepts`, `getInvestigation`, `createInvestigation`, `searchSources`, `listSearchProviders`, `fetchAndProcessSource`, `getSourceTasks`, `createReport`, `getReport`, `listReports`, `getReportTasks`. They expose the layered hierarchy directly: facts â†’ concepts â†’ summaries â†’ synthesis. An agent navigates *down* (`getConcept` â†’ `getRelatedConcepts` â†’ `getConcept` on a neighbor â†’ ...) and *up* (`searchFacts` â†’ `getFact` â†’ its linked concepts â†’ ...). Information-level tiering is enforced per agent: the research subagent does Phase 1 Plan (~20%) + Phase 2 Gather (~55%) + Phase 3 Validate/Repair/Report (~25%); the synthesizer does Phase 1 Broad Discovery (~20%) + Phase 2 Structural Mapping (~50%) + Phase 3 Deep Evidence Gathering (~25%) + Phase 4 Verify and Write (~5%) (Scope E Â§11).

**`searchFacts` `concepts` intersection is the verification primitive.** The handler `handleSearchFacts` (`mcp.go:724-876`) accepts a `concepts` parameter â€” a list of 2-20 concept names (bounds enforced at lines 785-807). After dedup by lowercased name, it resolves each concept to its group's concept_ids via `m.resolveConcept` and builds two parallel arrays: `allIDs []pgtype.UUID` (flattened concept_ids) and `allGroups []int32` (group index each concept_id belongs to). The SQL `ListSharedFactsByConceptGroups` (`backend/db/queries/facts.sql:420-468`) unnests both arrays in parallel with `WITH ORDINALITY`, joins them positionally by `c1.c1_idx = g2.g2_idx`, restricts to `fact_concepts` rows whose concept_id is in the input set, groups by `fact_id` and computes `COUNT(DISTINCT g2.gv) AS covered_groups`, then filters to facts whose `covered_groups` equals the total distinct group count â€” i.e. the fact is linked to at least one concept_id from *every* group. This is the **N-ary intersection**: "find facts at the intersection of these N concept groups." A single SQL query expresses a multi-concept verification that would require multiple joins or a client-side loop in a triplet KG (Scope A Â§10, Scope E Â§10).

## 5. Novel Contributions â€” Point 11 integrated

OKT's contribution is the **assembly**, not any single component (Scope C Â§11). The novel contributions are:

1. **Fact atoms (Point 1).** Decomposition into 9 typed atomic facts instead of chunks makes the unit of epistemic weight small, deduplicatable, and concept-linkable. The fact is the retrieval unit + evaluation unit + dedup unit + citation target, simultaneously and persistently â€” not ephemeral retrieval targets as in Dense X ([per Scope B](<fact:d6576607-ea8b-4bd6-af5e-fc335b9f18bd>)).
2. **Emergent ontology (Points 12, 13).** Concepts emerge from facts; no preexisting ontology must be authored or maintained. The context vocabulary is the only top-down element, and it is a closed, curated typing label (789 / 88 / ~30), not a relational schema. Cimiano ([concept, Scope C](<concept:b851fb5f-ef36-43c9-a6c3-1480b38cd011>)) learns ontologies; OKT drops the ontology and keeps the concept graph.
3. **Per-fact disambiguation (Point 2).** `ResolveAliasMatchForFact` decides concept membership per fact via cosine tie-break, not per-alias vote. Two facts in the same sentence that both use the surface form "N" can route to different concepts. This is structurally different from Kolitsas/GENRE/BLINK (per-mention, external KB, cross-encoder) â€” finer granularity, narrower scope.
4. **Provenance-preserving dedup (Point 4).** `mergeSources` preserves the union of both facts' sources, non-colliding sentence references, and concept links. A merge never loses provenance; the accumulation *compounds* across runs. Contrast with Barlaug & Gulla's open end-to-end integration gap ([per Scope C](<fact:c6975aea-6972-43a0-9050-a48cb9f52b66>)).
5. **Compounding substrate (Point 7).** Idempotent compounding (`AddFactSource` ON CONFLICT, `CreateFact` content hash, `fact_concepts` unique index, `concept_syntheses` unique on `(repo, lower(canonical_name))`) â€” re-fetching, re-processing, re-annotating are all no-ops on existing data. A research agent in Phase 2 can re-enter Phase 1 via `fetchAndProcessSource` (`Process: true`), and new facts compound into the existing pool. This is the architectural difference from one-shot RAG (no memory) and AI Scientist v1/v2 (ephemeral internal state).
6. **Auto-annotation as auditability (Point 9).** The 4-label posture classifier + hybrid lexical fallback + 0.6 apples-to-oranges gate makes the citation graph a first-class, queryable artifact. Unsupported prose is surfaced by *absence of annotations*, not by a separate verification pass. The system does not prevent hallucination; it surfaces it.
7. **Novel KG structure (Point 11).** Facts as atoms; concepts many-per-name; relations derived not asserted. The graph is a projection of the fact pool, always consistent with it by construction, with no separately authored structure whose judgment must be trusted. Contrast with triplet KGs (REBEL, Carta) where the KG *is* the asserted edge set.
8. **Multimodal facts (Point 8).** `fact_kind='image'` facts enter the same pipeline as text facts; charts and captions dedup against each other and share concepts. The synthesis image picker and `embeddedImageIDRe` let syntheses cite images with `![alt](<fact:uuid>)` markdown.

No single predecessor combines all eight. REBEL has triplet atoms but no emergent graph; GraphRAG has an emergent graph but no persistent fact pool; BLINK has disambiguation but no dedup-with-provenance; HiBERT has hierarchy but over documents not facts and without citation links; RAPTOR has recursive summarization but ephemeral retrieval-target output; OG-RAG has ontology-guided attribution but top-down not emergent; LightRAG has incremental update but transient answer output, not a persistent citable definition (Scope C Â§11).

## 6. Comparative Analysis â€” Point 10 (parallel scenarios, symmetric skepticism)

### 6.1 OKT vs RAG

**Scenario A (OKT's wager â€” atomic facts compound).** Dense X shows propositions outperform passages ([per Scope B](<fact:d6576607-ea8b-4bd6-af5e-fc335b9f18bd>)); FActScore decomposes into atomic facts to *evaluate* ([per Scope B](<fact:4d72c485-8c61-4602-838d-2d974eac7d4a>)); OKT persists propositions as durable rows indefinitely, dedups them cross-source with provenance unioned, and links them to emergent concepts. The second query is answered against a richer, deduplicated, concept-linked pool than the first.

**Scenario B (RAG's wager â€” context coherence at retrieval time).** Adaptive chunking beats proposition chunking on at least one benchmark ([per Scope B](<fact:de7e0df1-58d1-4c2a-bb48-acfe62f7c659>)); extraction is an LLM call that can drop qualifiers; RAGTruth shows extraction-LLM hallucination is a documented RAG-pipeline failure mode ([per Scope B](<fact:3a087c2e-bde2-41ef-925e-57b986ef3a27>)). RAG's per-query cost is roughly constant and there is no ingestion cost amortized over many queries â€” for single-query use cases RAG is structurally cheaper.

**Symmetric skepticism (Scope B Â§12).** Chunk-RAG's dominance rests on retrieval-task benchmarks, not on chunks as a good *persistent* unit. Proposition retrieval's superiority rests on QA-style tasks, not on long-horizon knowledge accumulation. FActScore's "reliable knowledge source" (Wikipedia) is itself curated, biased, and gap-ridden. RAGTruth's 43.1% rate uses a conservative annotation scheme that may inflate severity. OKT's wager â€” that a smaller, purpose-built, deduped, concept-linked pool is a better ground truth for a *specific research corpus* than the open web â€” is plausible but **empirical, not established**: it depends on ingestion coverage, extraction fidelity, and dedup threshold, none of which OKT has validated against an external gold standard. **Each system's claim is internally coherent and externally under-tested; none is entitled to default credibility.**

### 6.2 OKT vs triplet KGs (REBEL, Carta, OpenIE)

**Scenario A (typed relations are right).** Multi-hop reasoning over a structured KB requires typed paths. SubgraphRAG ([per Scope C](<fact:895adca5-b30a-4464-b578-8a65a3e4729b>)) "improves efficiency, accuracy, and reliability by reducing hallucinations" on WebQSP/CWQ. ToG ([per Scope C](<fact:1bff2bed-0023-4242-a47f-1493dec61ebb>)) iteratively executes beam search on a KG. Both rely on typed paths for traversal; without predicates there is no path, only co-occurrence. On this reading, OKT's matview is a similarity co-occurrence structure, not a reasoning substrate.

**Scenario B (derived relations are right).** Every asserted triple can be wrong, and extraction errors propagate downstream with no fact pool to audit against. REBEL's own synthesis concedes exposure bias and that "a relation's presence in Wikidata does not guarantee it is entailed in the specific text" ([REBEL concept, Scope C](<concept:5a9fe41f-aa2a-4b0f-881c-ecfc72368e1f>)). OKT's matview is `COUNT(DISTINCT fact_id)`: always consistent with the fact pool by construction. The cost is representational â€” no "X causes Y" as a typed edge; the benefit is auditability: every edge traces to fact rows that trace to source URLs.

**When each is appropriate (Scope C Â§5).** Triplet KGs suit typed-path multi-hop reasoning over structured KBs (WebQSP, CWQ, Freebase-style). OKT suits fact-level verification and citation in domains with no pre-built ontology where the goal is auditability rather than path traversal. Neither dominates; they optimize different objective functions.

### 6.3 OKT vs top-down ontology (DBpedia, Wikidata, OG-RAG)

**Scenario A (top-down is necessary/superior).** Typed schemas are machine-readable, queryable by type, federated, interoperable. Wikidata's qualifier machinery expresses ternary relations cleanly ([per Scope C](<fact:a20d3785-d262-4272-a454-a8a072ae493b>)); its "no value"/"unknown" distinction prevents "common neighbor" query errors. OG-RAG "achieves the highest correctness scores across Soybean (0.84), Wheat (0.82), and News (0.52), outperforming the RAG, RAPTOR, and GraphRAG methods" ([per Scope C](<fact:e1c17491-3c3f-4083-8ece-fe568978d375>)) â€” the strongest single piece of evidence in the pool *for* the top-down pole, and OKT has no comparable benchmark result to set against it.

**Scenario B (emergent is necessary/superior).** Any predefined schema excludes what does not fit. DBpedia's free-form relation naming produces synonym duplication ([per Scope C](<fact:07b3ed42-dea7-4485-9506-5615c76244f2>)); its consistency is unknown ([per Scope C](<fact:56c86c85-033e-4582-8180-c8b7d8d70778>)). Cimiano concedes by *learning* ontologies. OKT generalizes further: no schema at all except context vocabulary, and it is a typing label, not a relational schema.

**Where they converge/diverge (Scope C Â§4).** Both extract from text; both can be LLM-driven. The discriminating experiment â€” has anyone shown that a maximally-emergent graph answers the same class of questions as a typed KG with comparable accuracy? â€” is **open**. OKT sits at the maximally-emergent end; its context vocabulary is the only top-down residue.

### 6.4 OKT vs GraphRAG / LightRAG / SubgraphRAG / ToG / OG-RAG

- **GraphRAG** (Edge et al. 2024 / Larson & Truitt 2024, Microsoft Research, [per Scope D](<fact:5d1efbd1-2781-44de-abb4-1c32891f9425>)) builds an entity graph in two stages and pregenerates community summaries for clusters of related entities. Closest structural cousin. OKT differs: community summaries are cluster-shaped, OKT's syntheses are entity-shaped (one per canonical-name group); GraphRAG is built for global sensemaking, OKT for per-concept questions plus fact-level verification.
- **LightRAG** ([per Scope C](<fact:c18c6b79-f7e3-4d46-a52d-bd2e6e47f964>); [per Scope C](<fact:80d6a01b-e6e8-4b79-8d90-670a37fd4b93>)) has an incremental update algorithm â€” the closest architectural relative to OKT's compounding. But LightRAG's output is still a transient answer, not a persistent citable definition.
- **SubgraphRAG/ToG** require a pre-existing typed KG â€” no OKT analogue, because OKT has no typed predicates.
- **OG-RAG** is opposite on the ontology axis: top-down ontology-guided, where OKT is maximally emergent. The OG-RAG correctness gains ([per Scope C](<fact:e1c17491-3c3f-4083-8ece-fe568978d375>)) are the strongest single piece of evidence in the pool *for* the top-down pole.

**Parallel scenarios â€” community summaries vs per-concept syntheses (Scope C Â§8).** *Scenario A (GraphRAG):* the unit is the *community*; the LLM builds an entity graph, pregenerates community summaries, runs map-reduce for global questions; output is ephemeral and rebuilt per corpus. *Scenario B (OKT):* the unit is the *concept group*; summary slices (BatchSize=20, one LLM call per slice, batch-only mode) feed a per-canonical-name group synthesis that is **persistent**, **compounds** across runs (no-delta skip), and is returned by `getConcept` as an **authoritative citable definition** with `[text](<fact:fact_id>)` links. **Neither is "better."** GraphRAG is optimized for global cross-entity questions; OKT for per-concept questions plus fact-level verification.

### 6.5 OKT vs AI Scientist v1/v2

AI Scientist v1/v2 **is** an agent â€” it carries ephemeral internal state across idea generation, experimental iteration, write-up, and review, and emits a finished artifact; its intermediate state is not structured as a queryable graph that survives the run. OKT **is not an agent** â€” it is a knowledge-building pipeline (Phase 1) plus an agent-navigation substrate (Phase 2). The two are not substitutes; an AI Scientist-style agent could in principle be built **on top of** OKT's MCP surface, replacing v1/v2's ephemeral scratchpad with the persistent fact/concept graph (Scope D Â§4). **(Agent hypothesis, not stated by any source: the v1/v2 reported failure modes â€” incorrect implementations, unfair baselines, magnitude errors, citation hallucinations, code never run â€” are exactly the class a verifiable fact-pool substrate is designed to surface. Confirming this would require an AI-Scientist-on-OKT variant (Scope D Â§4).)**

### 6.6 OKT vs FActScore / SelfCheckGPT

OKT's auto-annotation is functionally closest to FActScore â€” both segment a generation into atomic units and check each against a pool â€” but OKT's pool is a **curated internal fact pool built from the same domain** (the repo's `okt_repository.facts` table), not open-web Wikipedia. This is the structural reason to predict OKT wins in-domain and loses out-of-domain (Experiment 2). SelfCheckGPT takes the opposite design choice: it requires no external pool but is structurally blind to consistently-made false claims (a hallucination stated identically across samples passes SelfCheckGPT). The two are complements on different failure modes, not substitutes on the same one (Scope D Â§5).

## 7. Strengths and Weaknesses â€” Point 14 (honest)

### 7.1 Strengths (Scope A Â§14)

- **Atomic facts.** Decomposition into 9 typed atomic facts (`builtin_prompts.go:22-169`) instead of chunks makes the unit of epistemic weight small, deduplicatable, and concept-linkable.
- **Emergent ontology.** Concepts emerge from facts; no preexisting ontology must be authored or maintained. The context vocabulary is the only top-down element, and it is a closed, curated list.
- **Per-fact disambiguation.** `ResolveAliasMatchForFact` decides concept membership per fact via cosine tie-break, not per-alias vote.
- **Sentence-level provenance.** `[Sn]` prefixes + `fact_references` rows give every fact a traceable path back to specific sentences in specific sources.
- **Dedup-preserves-all-references guarantee.** `mergeSources` preserves the union of both facts' sources, non-colliding sentence references, and concept links. A merge never loses provenance.
- **Auto-annotation with posture.** The 4-label posture classifier labels each (sentence, fact) pair as supports/contradicts/related/irrelevant and drops irrelevant pairs before persistence. The auditability property follows by absence.
- **Multimodal facts.** `fact_kind='image'` facts enter the same pipeline as text facts; charts and captions dedup against each other and share concepts.
- **Idempotent compounding.** Re-fetching, re-processing, and re-annotating are all no-ops on existing data. Compounding is safe.
- **Layered agent navigation.** The 16 MCP tools expose the facts â†’ concepts â†’ summaries â†’ synthesis hierarchy directly, and the `searchFacts` `concepts` intersection (2-20) is a verification primitive no triplet KG offers in one query.

### 7.2 Weaknesses (Scope A Â§14, honest limits in B/C/D)

- **LLM-dependent extraction quality.** The 9-type fact prompt and the concept extraction prompt are the only things between a source and the fact pool. A fact the extraction model missed is invisible to every downstream stage; a fact it extracted badly is a bad atom in the pool forever (until reaped by 168h `fact_catchup`). No fallback verifies extraction recall. RAGTruth's 43.1% rate ([per Scope B](<fact:3a087c2e-bde2-41ef-925e-57b986ef3a27>)) shows extraction-LLM hallucination is a documented RAG-pipeline failure mode; OKT is not immune.
- **Dedup threshold is a tuning knob.** `0.94` cosine is a single global threshold. The `dedup-threshold-sweep` experiment shows the `to_delete` rate varies sharply with threshold; one value cannot be optimal across both dense and heterogeneous corpora. A false merge (threshold too low) unions citations from distinct facts, producing a fact row whose source list over-claims.
- **Embedding model bounds disambiguation.** The per-fact cosine tie-break in `ResolveAliasMatchForFact` is only as good as the `gemini-embedding-2` 3072-dim representation. If two senses of a concept name embed near each other, disambiguation fails silently. No cross-encoder re-rank to recover specificity the way BLINK does ([per Scope C](<fact:998e5217-4765-44e2-b8f4-6bec84e0295e>)).
- **Concept canonicalization depends on the refinement LLM.** The refinement worker is **off by default** (`config.default.yaml:633`) and gated by a per-canonical advisory lock. If an operator never enables it, `concept_candidates` accumulate unresolved; a bad canonical name chosen at creation pollutes the `(repository_id, lower(canonical_name))` group forever after, because `concept_syntheses` is unique on that key.
- **Cost.** One LLM call per chunk-batch (decomposition), per concept batch (extraction), per refinement candidate, per summary slice, per synthesis, per image picker, per posture batch. A 100-source ingestion can produce thousands of LLM calls. The summarization batch-only mode and the synthesis no-delta skip are the only cost caps.
- **No explicit predicate relations.** `concept_relations` is a pure co-occurrence count. "X causes Y" is not expressible as a queryable graph edge; it surfaces only as synthesis prose. SubgraphRAG/ToG-style traversal is not available (Scope C Â§11).
- **Emergent ontology can fragment without aggressive pruning.** The `prune_threshold: 5` re-prune gate only fires when 5 new aliases accumulate. With refinement off, two near-identical concepts can coexist indefinitely, fragmenting `shared_fact_count` across both.
- **Qdrant dependency.** Qdrant is not a transaction participant. A Qdrant outage degrades search but not storage; a Postgres-commit-then-Qdrant-failure window leaves vectors stale until the next embedding pass. The system is *eventually* consistent between Postgres and Qdrant, not atomically.
- **Posture classifier can mislabel.** The 4-label classifier inherits the LLM's judgment; AttributionBench places an ~80% F1 ceiling on this kind of posture classification ([per Scope B](<fact:6c3627b2-22bc-4436-b4cf-33a661ebb286>)), so ~1 in 5 labels is wrong. A mislabel is silent: the annotation row persists with a wrong `posture` and no human review is in the loop. RAGTruth's authors claim "a fine-tuned small Llama-2-13B can outperform GPT-4 prompted for the same task" ([per Scope D](<fact:02f2e394-e178-4e94-bfad-3d49bd22f9f3>)) â€” a direct challenge to OKT's prompt-based posture classifier.
- **No generation-time prevention.** OKT does not prevent hallucination at generation time. It surfaces unsupported prose post-hoc by failing to find citations. A sentence that hallucinates a claim *also in the pool* will be annotated as supported; a sentence that makes a true claim *absent from the pool* will be flagged (Scope B Â§9).
- **Fact pool is biased toward whatever has been ingested.** A curated deduped pool is smaller and biased but deduplicated, concept-linked, and compounding; the open web covers everything but is noisy, redundant, and non-compounding. OKT has not validated its pool against an external gold standard.

### 7.3 Honest framing

The architecture is therefore not a hallucination *preventer*. It is a hallucination *surfacer*: a layered system in which every claim can be traced to a fact, every fact to its sources and sentences, every concept relation to the shared facts that derive it, and every report sentence to its supporting annotations or its visible absence of them (Scope A Â§14). The fragility lives in the LLM calls that gate each layer â€” extraction, disambiguation, refinement, summarization, synthesis, posture â€” and in the operator-tunable thresholds (`0.94`, `0.7`, `0.6`, `5`, `20`, `10`) that determine where each layer's decisions land. The code paths are the ground truth; the strengths and weaknesses inherit from which decisions are schema-enforced (the `facts` table has no `source_id`, the matview has no predicates) and which are prompt- or threshold-enforced (extraction quality, posture labels, dedup boundary).

## 8. Proposed Benchmarks â€” Point 15

Six experiments (Scope D Part 2). For each: (a) Hypothesis, (b) Systems compared, (c) Dataset, (d) Metric, (e) Protocol naming actual OKT MCP tools/endpoints, (f) Confounders with symmetric controls, (g) Parallel-scenario outcomes stated in advance. **General control (applies to all six):** fix the embedding model (`google/gemini-embedding-2`, 3072-dim) and the extraction/synthesis LLM (`google/gemma-4-31b-it` for extraction and concept-extraction; `deepseek/deepseek-v4-flash:turbo` for synthesis) across **both** OKT and any baseline. If OKT wins because its embedding is better, that is an embedding-vendor result, not an architecture result.

### 8.1 Experiment 1 â€” Fact-level vs chunk-level retrieval precision

**(a) Hypothesis.** Atomic-fact retrieval (OKT `searchFacts`) yields higher precision@k than chunk-level retrieval (LlamaIndex `VectorStoreIndex` with `SentenceSplitter(chunk_size=512, chunk_overlap=50)`) for specific factual questions.

**(b) Systems.** OKT `searchFacts` MCP tool (full-text + Qdrant hybrid via `websearch_to_tsquery` + vector search) vs LlamaIndex `VectorStoreIndex` over the same PDFs with the fixed embedding.

**(c) Dataset.** 50-100 scientific papers + 50 held-out factual questions with a gold answer = a specific atomic fact present in one paper. Single-fact questions.

**(d) Metric.** Precision@1, @3, @5, @10; citation precision (for OKT: does `getFact(factId)` return a `fact_sources` row whose source URL matches the gold paper? for LlamaIndex: does the retrieved chunk's source file match?).

**(e) Protocol.** (1) Ingest via `fetchAndProcessSource(url=...)`, one MCP call per paper, `Process: true`. (2) Drain: poll `getSourceTasks(repository, verbose=false, limit=200)` per the drain protocol, paging every `next_cursor` accumulating `pending_count`; declare drained only when the final page has `next_cursor` empty AND `pending_count=0`; verify `synthesize_concept` jobs completed via `getSourceTasks(byKind=true)`. (3) Build a LlamaIndex `VectorStoreIndex` over the same PDFs with the fixed embedding. (4) For each of 50 questions: call `searchFacts(repository, query=q, limit=10)`; record top-10 fact IDs; call `getFact` on each; score precision@k and citation precision. Simultaneously query the LlamaIndex index for top-10 chunks. (5) Report per-question and aggregate precision@k.

**(f) Confounders.** OKT extraction quality: audit a 50-fact random sample for self-containedness and correctness. LlamaIndex chunk_size sweep {256, 512, 1024}; report best. Same embedding both sides. Stratify single-fact vs multi-sentence-context.

**(g) Parallel-scenario outcomes.** *OKT-favourable:* precision@1 â‰« LlamaIndex on single-fact; citation precision = 1.0 (mechanical â€” `getFact` always returns source URL). *Baseline-favourable:* LlamaIndex wins recall on multi-sentence-context (the surrounding prose provides context the bare atomic fact lacks).

**Honest prediction.** OKT wins precision@1 on single-fact; LlamaIndex wins recall on synthesis. This is a design prediction, not a performance claim.

### 8.2 Experiment 2 â€” Hallucination detection via auto-annotation vs SelfCheckGPT/FActScore

**(a) Hypothesis.** OKT `annotate_report` (`similarity_threshold=0.7` config / `0.84` code fallback, `lexical_similarity_floor=0.6`, posture on, `google/gemma-4-31b-it` classifier, batch 8 concurrent 4) detects inserted hallucinations at higher F1 than SelfCheckGPT and FActScore, *for claims in the OKT fact pool's domain*.

**(b) Systems.** OKT `createReport` + `getReport` vs SelfCheckGPT (sample N=5 at T=1.0) vs FActScore (`pip install factscore`, Wikipedia).

**(c) Dataset.** 20 human-written reports ~1000 words each in the OKT default repo's domain; 10 with 5 deliberate hallucinated sentences each, 10 clean. Sentence-level ground truth hand-labeled.

**(d) Metric.** Per-sentence P/R/F1. OKT rule: flagged if zero `report_annotations` OR highest-score annotation has `posture=contradicts`. SelfCheckGPT: flagged if inter-sample consistency below threshold. FActScore: flagged if any atomic fact unsupported by Wikipedia.

**(e) Protocol.** (1) Confirm corpus ingested and pool drained. (2) For each of 20 reports: `createReport(repository, title, text=report_body)`; poll `getReportTasks(repository, reportId, verbose=false)` until `complete=true` and `pending_count=0`; `getReport(repository, reportId)` to read `report_annotations`. (3) Run SelfCheckGPT: sample N=5 at T=1.0, compute per-sentence consistency. (4) Run FActScore: decompose into atomic facts, check against Wikipedia (and a second run against Wikipedia+Google). (5) Score all three against ground truth.

**(f) Confounders.** OKT `similarity_threshold` sweep {0.78, 0.82, 0.84, 0.88}. SelfCheckGPT N sweep {3, 5, 10}. FActScore with Wikipedia-only and Wikipedia+Google. OKT posture ON vs OFF (clean ablation). Out-of-corpus claims: deliberately include 5 hallucinated sentences whose content is *outside* the OKT pool's domain to test the out-of-domain failure mode.

**(g) Parallel-scenario outcomes.** *OKT-favourable:* F1 > both baselines on in-domain; high precision on `contradicts`-flagged; high recall on uncited. *Baseline-favourable:* SelfCheckGPT and FActScore win on out-of-corpus claims (OKT's pool does not cover them â€” a true claim outside the pool is flagged = false positive; a hallucinated claim outside the pool is also flagged = the flag is uninformative).

**Honest prediction.** OKT wins in-domain, loses out-of-domain. The curated-pool design choice is high-precision inside its scope and blind outside; SelfCheckGPT's sampling-variance approach is domain-agnostic but blind to consistently-made false claims. The two are complements on different failure modes.

### 8.3 Experiment 3 â€” Dedup quality vs naive exact-match / embedding-cluster dedup

**(a) Hypothesis.** OKT's stable-wins + `mergeSources` dedup (threshold 0.94 cosine) preserves more citation density on survivors than exact-hash dedup and DBSCAN-cluster dedup, without excessive false merges.

**(b) Systems.** OKT `deduplicate_facts` (the live worker) vs exact-hash dedup (SHA-256 of normalized fact text) vs scikit-learn DBSCAN on the fact embeddings (eps = OKT threshold).

**(c) Dataset.** 20 review articles + 20 primary studies on the same topic.

**(d) Metric.** (i) Distinct facts after dedup; (ii) citation density on survivors (avg `fact_sources` count, avg `fact_references` count); (iii) manual audit of a 100-pair random sample: false-merge rate and false-keep rate.

**(e) Protocol.** (1) The existing scaffolding `scripts/experiments/dedup-threshold-sweep/dedup_threshold_sweep.py` connects directly to Qdrant (6333 REST) and Postgres (5432), replays the `deduplicate_facts` nearest-neighbor search for every embedded fact of a single investigation, and sweeps thresholds 0.80-0.98. Read-only. Reports two views: any-neighbor (upper bound) and cross-source-only (matches the live worker). Existing report: `reports/dedup_sweep_shadow_fleet.html` (139 sources, 4,427 embedded text-stable facts, 12.5s sweep). (2) Extend to run three strategies â€” OKT stable-wins, exact-hash, DBSCAN â€” on the same fact set. (3) For the 100-pair manual audit: two labelers independently classify each pair as "same fact" / "different fact"; Cohen's Îº; disagreements adjudicated by a third labeler.

**(f) Confounders.** OKT threshold sweep {0.86, 0.90, 0.94}. DBSCAN shares the OKT threshold as `eps`. 2 labelers + inter-annotator agreement. Same embedding both sides.

**(g) Parallel-scenario outcomes.** *OKT-favourable:* citation density higher via `mergeSources` (mechanical â€” `mergeSources` unions `fact_sources` and preserves non-overlapping `fact_references`, so density strictly cannot decrease on a true merge); false-merge rate lower than DBSCAN at matched threshold. *Baseline-favourable:* DBSCAN achieves comparable citation density at lower compute; OKT false-merge rate non-trivial at 0.94 (the threshold is global; some fact pairs at 0.94 cosine are genuinely different).

**Honest prediction.** OKT wins citation density â€” mechanical consequence of `mergeSources`, not empirical. False-merge rate is genuinely uncertain; the threshold-sweep scaffolding will show the precision-recall curve, but the 100-pair audit is the only way to see whether 0.94 is well-calibrated for the test domain.

### 8.4 Experiment 4 â€” Emergent concept quality vs top-down ontology mapping

**(a) Hypothesis.** OKT's emergent concept set covers more of the entities a human annotator identifies than top-down DBpedia Spotlight, at the cost of higher fragmentation.

**(b) Systems.** OKT `searchConcepts(limit=200)` vs DBpedia Spotlight (confidence 0.5) vs a Wikidata linker (mGENRE or BLINK).

**(c) Dataset.** The 50-100-paper corpus from Experiment 1 + 30 hand-built shared-alias test cases ("N" â†’ Nitrogen/Neutron/Nano/Number; "Washington" â†’ person/city/state; "Mercury" â†’ element/planet/god/brand).

**(d) Metric.** (i) Coverage (% of gold entities found); (ii) fragmentation (avg system-IDs per gold entity; 1 = ideal); (iii) disambiguation accuracy on the 30 shared-alias cases.

**(e) Protocol.** (1) The existing scaffolding `scripts/experiments/disambiguation_benchmark.py` (1238 lines) tests whether the 88-category `manual_select.json` preserves the disambiguation power of the 789-label `dbpedia_l3.json`. It crafts ambiguous facts, sends each to `google/gemma-4-31b-it` via OpenRouter twice (original vs candidate list), compares (concept, context) pairs. Output: `disambiguation_benchmark.json`. (2) Extend to run three systems â€” OKT concept extraction (the live `extract_concepts` worker), DBpedia Spotlight, Wikidata linker â€” on the same corpus. (3) For OKT disambiguation specifically: the per-fact `ResolveAliasMatchForFact` helper uses cosine tie-break between the fact's Qdrant vector and each candidate concept's vector when an alias is shared. The 30 shared-alias cases directly exercise this path. (4) One human annotator, one gold entity list, all three systems (symmetric annotation).

**(f) Confounders.** OKT context vocabulary choice: run with DBpedia L3 (789), `manual_select.json` (88), and the scientific preset (~30); report all three. Spotlight confidence sweep {0.3, 0.5, 0.7}. Same human annotator. The 30 shared-alias cases reported separately.

**(g) Parallel-scenario outcomes.** *OKT-favourable:* coverage > Spotlight (emergent set includes corpus-specific entities no top-down ontology has); disambiguation > Spotlight on shared-alias (per-fact embedding tie-break uses the *fact's* context, not just the alias string). *Baseline-favourable:* Spotlight + Wikidata win disambiguation via pre-built ontology + alias maps (Wikidata's alias maps are human-curated and encyclopedic); OKT fragmentation higher (the `(canonical_name, context)` identity is more granular than a single DBpedia URI).

**Honest prediction.** OKT wins coverage (mechanical â€” emergent extraction cannot miss a corpus-specific entity that a top-down ontology does not have); OKT loses fragmentation (mechanical â€” more granular identity). Disambiguation is genuinely uncertain â€” OKT's per-fact embedding tie-break is a clever mechanism, but Wikidata's curated alias maps are an entirely different and very strong mechanism. The 30-case shared-alias test is the discriminating experiment.

### 8.5 Experiment 5 â€” Compounding vs one-shot RAG over a growing corpus

**(a) Hypothesis.** OKT's marginal LLM cost per new source decreases as the corpus grows (cache hits on existing concepts, `BatchSize=20`-gated summary skip), while one-shot RAG's marginal cost is roughly constant, and OKT answer quality on a fixed test set improves faster per marginal dollar.

**(b) Systems.** OKT (ingest one source at a time, measure after each) vs LlamaIndex re-build-from-scratch after each source vs LlamaIndex incremental-insert after each source.

**(c) Dataset.** The 50-100-paper corpus in a fixed random order + a fixed set of 20 questions (mixed single-fact and synthesis).

**(d) Metric per newly-ingested source.** (i) LLM tokens consumed (OKT: `okt_system.ai_usage`; LlamaIndex: embedding API count + tokens); (ii) wall-clock drain time; (iii) precision@5 on the 20 questions; (iv) OKT graph density (avg `shared_fact_count` from the `concept_relations` matview); (v) OKT summary freshness (fraction of concepts whose `concept_summaries.is_complete=true` and whose `concept_syntheses` row exists).

**(e) Protocol.** (1) Fix a random order. (2) For source `i` in 1..N: (a) OKT: `fetchAndProcessSource(url=paper_i_url)`. Full drain protocol. Record `okt_system.ai_usage` delta, wall-clock, graph density after drain. Run the 20 questions via `searchFacts`; record precision@5. (b) LlamaIndex rebuild: insert into a fresh `VectorStoreIndex` each time. (c) LlamaIndex incremental: insert into the existing `VectorStoreIndex`. (3) Plot per-source cost, cumulative cost, precision@5, and (for OKT) graph density and summary freshness, as functions of `i`.

**(f) Confounders.** OKT `BatchSize=20` makes cost bursty â€” report a **rolling 20-source average** of tokens-per-source, not the per-source value. LlamaIndex gets two baselines (rebuild = worst case, incremental = fair case). Same embedding, same order, same questions.

**(g) Parallel-scenario outcomes.** *OKT-favourable:* rolling-average tokens-per-source trends downward as `i` grows; precision@5 rises faster per cumulative token; graph density rises monotonically. *Baseline-favourable:* LlamaIndex-incremental also has roughly constant per-source cost and lower per-source cost than OKT (no LLM extraction step); precision@5 comparable.

**Honest prediction.** OKT's per-source cost will **not** cleanly trend downward enough to beat LlamaIndex-incremental on cost alone â€” OKT pays an LLM extraction cost per source that LlamaIndex does not. The real question is whether precision@5 rises *faster per cumulative token* â€” whether the structured graph produces more answer-quality per dollar than the flat index. This is the experiment's discriminating question; the cost curve alone is not.

### 8.6 Experiment 6 â€” GraphRAG-style global QA vs OKT concept synthesis

**(a) Hypothesis (corrected per Scope D Â§12).** OKT's `getConcept` (synthesis) + `getRelatedConcepts` + `searchFacts(concepts=[X,Y])` yields comparable comprehensiveness and diversity to Microsoft GraphRAG's community-summary approach. **Correction flagged:** GraphRAG in fact builds community summaries at *indexing* time, not query time, so the per-query token cost comparison is "OKT reads pre-built syntheses vs GraphRAG reads pre-built community summaries." The honest hypothesis is about comprehensiveness/diversity/grounding, not indexing-vs-query-time cost.

**(b) Systems.** OKT (`searchConcepts` â†’ `getConcept` â†’ `getRelatedConcepts` â†’ `searchFacts(concepts=[X,Y])`) vs Microsoft GraphRAG (`graphrag query --method global`).

**(c) Dataset.** The 50-100-paper corpus + 20 global/theme questions.

**(d) Metric.** GraphRAG's own comprehensiveness + diversity metrics (LLM-as-judge pairwise) + token cost per question (OKT: `ai_usage` delta; GraphRAG: run logs) + grounding (human annotator: does the answer cite facts that exist in the corpus? for OKT checkable via `[text](<fact:fact_id>)` citations; for GraphRAG requires tracing community summaries back to source entities).

**(e) Protocol.** (1) Ingest into OKT and run the full drain protocol. Critically, confirm `synthesize_concept` jobs completed via `getSourceTasks(byKind=true)` â€” OKT's syntheses only exist when those jobs finalize. (2) Build a GraphRAG index over the same corpus (`graphrag index`). Report GraphRAG indexing cost separately. (3) For each of 20 global questions: (a) OKT: run the navigation pattern `searchConcepts(query=question_terms) â†’ getConcept(top_concept) â†’ getRelatedConcepts(top_concept) â†’ searchFacts(concepts=[top_concept, related_concept])`; assemble an answer. (b) GraphRAG: `graphrag query --method global --query question`. (c) Record `ai_usage` delta and run logs. (4) LLM-as-judge pairwise: blind, randomized order, same judge model. (5) Human annotator: check whether cited facts/entities exist in the corpus.

**(f) Confounders.** OKT syntheses only exist when `synthesize_concept` jobs complete â€” confirm before running queries. GraphRAG indexing cost reported separately from per-query cost. Same LLM for the judge and for any LLM calls. Question-type stratification: 10 "single-theme" (one concept cluster) and 10 "cross-theme" (multiple clusters); report per-stratum.

**(g) Parallel-scenario outcomes.** *OKT-favourable:* â‰¥ GraphRAG on comprehensiveness/diversity; lower per-query token cost (reading pre-built syntheses vs running a global query that re-summarizes); higher grounding (mechanical â€” OKT syntheses carry `[text](<fact:fact_id>)` citations, GraphRAG community summaries trace to entities not facts). *Baseline-favourable:* GraphRAG wins comprehensiveness/diversity for fresh corpora (community detection finds thematic clusters that emergent entity extraction may not surface as concepts); GraphRAG indexing cost lower than OKT Phase 1.

**Honest prediction.** OKT most likely **loses** on comprehensiveness/diversity for fresh corpora â€” GraphRAG is purpose-built for global sensemaking, and its community-detection thematic clustering is a structural advantage for cross-cutting theme questions that OKT's entity-shaped syntheses are not designed for. OKT should win **grounding** (mechanical citation advantage). Per-query cost is uncertain. Question-type-dependent: single-theme favors OKT; cross-theme favors GraphRAG.

### 8.7 Significance (Scope D Â§13)

The six experiments ask "which architectural commitment of OKT pays off against which baseline, and at what cost," not "is OKT better." The honest predictions reflect a design expecting OKT to win on **fact-level precision** (Experiment 1), **citation density** (Experiment 3 â€” mechanical), and **grounding** (Experiment 6 â€” mechanical citation advantage); to be **competitive on compounding cost-per-quality** (Experiment 5 â€” empirical, with the real question being quality-per-dollar); and to be **disadvantaged on cross-cutting theme QA** (Experiment 6) and **out-of-corpus hallucination detection** (Experiment 2). Disambiguation (Experiment 4) is genuinely uncertain. The parallel-scenario framing ensures that **either** outcome is reportable as a finding: an OKT win is a finding that the architectural commitment pays off there; a baseline win is a finding that it does not. No experiment is designed to be un-falsifiable in either direction.

## 9. Discussion

### 9.1 Where the evidence lands

Four literatures converge on two intuitions (Scope B Â§13): meaning lives at granularity finer than the document, and trust lives in the connection between a claim and its evidence. RAG discovered that fine-grained passage retrieval helps ([per Scope B](<fact:84412fe6-5b77-4c25-bec5-aa03ba457c18>)); Dense X discovered that propositions help more ([per Scope B](<fact:d6576607-ea8b-4bd6-af5e-fc335b9f18bd>)); FActScore discovered that atomic-fact evaluation is more informative than whole-text scoring ([per Scope B](<fact:4d72c485-8c61-4602-838d-2d974eac7d4a>)); Self-RAG discovered that the model should self-critique at generation time ([per Scope B](<fact:03bed1aa-e350-480a-87d6-950d525fce2c>)). OKT's contribution is the wager that these should be the **same persisted objects**: the atomic fact as retrieval unit + evaluation unit + dedup unit + citation target, compounding over time, with an emergent concept layer linking facts that no single source connected.

The combination â€” atomic persistence + cross-source dedup + emergent concept linking + sentence-level provenance + post-hoc grounding annotation â€” is not anticipated by any single prior system reviewed across the five scopes. Whether it outperforms open-web post-hoc attribution (RARR) or generation-time reflection (Self-RAG) on a research-corpus auditability task is an **open empirical question, not a settled claim**; the evidence reviewed supports OKT as a *plausible* candidate solution to AI hallucination and auditability, not *the* solution (Scope B Â§13).

### 9.2 Open questions

- **Is the maximally-emergent graph as queryable as a typed KG?** Scope C Â§4: the discriminating experiment is open; no fact in the pool directly tests it. OKT sits at the maximally-emergent end; OG-RAG's correctness gains ([per Scope C](<fact:e1c17491-3c3f-4083-8ece-fe568978d375>)) are the strongest single piece of evidence in the pool *for* the top-down pole.
- **Is the curated pool a better ground truth than the open web for a specific research corpus?** Plausible but empirical â€” depends on ingestion coverage, extraction fidelity, dedup threshold, none validated against an external gold standard (Scope B Â§12).
- **Does per-fact embedding tie-break beat Wikidata's curated alias maps on shared-alias cases?** Genuinely uncertain; Experiment 4 is the discriminating test (Scope D Â§10).
- **Does asynchronous-batched synergy (OKT) or per-token synergy (Self-RAG) produce better auditability per dollar?** Open (Scope D Â§3); Experiment 2 addresses one slice.
- **Can an AI-Scientist-style agent be built on OKT's MCP surface, and would the v1/v2 failure modes (incorrect implementations, unfair baselines, magnitude errors, citation hallucinations, code never run) be surfacable by the fact pool + auto-annotation?** Structural rhyme, not a tested result (Scope D Â§4).

### 9.3 Intellectual-honesty framing

This paper does not claim OKT is *the* solution. It claims OKT is *a potential* solution, supported by an architectural assembly whose components each have precedent in the literature but whose combination is novel, and whose design wager is falsifiable by six experiments runnable on existing scaffolding. The paper applies **symmetric skepticism** to OKT and to every baseline: no system is granted authority by reputation, and no design choice is treated as settled by repetition. OKT is a hallucination *surfacer*, not a hallucination *preventer*; the fragility lives in the LLM calls that gate each layer and in the operator-tunable thresholds that determine where each layer's decisions land. The reader compares the parallel scenarios; the experiments settle the wager.

## 10. Conclusion

The Open Knowledge Tree is a compounding knowledge substrate that decomposes sources into atomic facts, deduplicates them with provenance preserved, induces an emergent concept graph whose only top-down element is a curated context vocabulary, synthesizes per-concept definitions that persist and compound across runs, and auto-annotates reports against the fact pool so that every sentence carries its supporting facts or is visibly uncited. The architecture is a hallucination *surfacer*, not a preventer; its claim to address AI hallucination and auditability during scientific research rests on making the citation graph a first-class, queryable artifact rather than a post-hoc explanation. The thesis â€” that an emergent ontology plus a semantic knowledge graph is *a potential* solution â€” is falsifiable: six experiments with runnable protocols and parallel-scenario predictions settle whether each architectural commitment pays off against its named baseline. Honest predictions: OKT should win on fact-level precision, citation density, and grounding; be competitive on compounding cost-per-quality; and lose on cross-cutting theme QA and out-of-corpus hallucination detection. The architecture is a design wager with evidence for and against; the proposed benchmarks settle it.
