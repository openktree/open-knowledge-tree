# Scope C — Knowledge-Graph Construction, Ontology, and Retrieval-Augmented Generation: Situating OKT in the Comparative Literature

## 1. Opening

The Open Knowledge Tree (OKT) is, at its core, a system that ingests documents, emits **facts** (atomic text rows with provenance), grows an **emergent concept graph** in which edges are *derived* from shared facts rather than asserted as typed predicates, **disambiguates** shared aliases per-fact by embedding-cosine against the repository's own concept set, **deduplicates** facts by embedding distance while relinking the loser's sources/references/concepts onto the winner, and **summarizes** the pool in two tiers — per-concept summary slices that feed a per-canonical-group synthesis returned as an authoritative definition. None of these five primitives is unprecedented in isolation. Each has a well-developed body of predecessor literature: knowledge-graph construction from text, top-down versus emergent ontology, entity linking/disambiguation via embeddings, deduplication and record linkage, GraphRAG-family retrieval-augmented systems, and hierarchical multi-document summarization. This scope situates OKT within those six bodies and maps where it converges, diverges, and is silent. The thesis under comparison — that an emergent ontology plus a semantic knowledge graph is a candidate solution to AI hallucination and auditability in scientific research — is tested by holding OKT's design choices against the strongest available evidence for and against each alternative. Throughout, both OKT and its predecessors receive symmetric skepticism: no system is granted authority by reputation, and no design choice is treated as settled by repetition.

## 2. Scope & Boundaries

**In scope:** (a) knowledge-graph construction from text, (b) top-down versus emergent ontology, (c) entity disambiguation via embeddings, (d) deduplication and record linkage, (e) GraphRAG and competitor retrieval-augmented systems, (f) hierarchical multi-document summarization. **Out of scope:** the RAG-and-hallucination evidence base (treated in Scope B) and OKT's internal architecture and implementation (treated in Scope A). Where OKT internals are referenced here, it is only to anchor a comparison to an external method, not to re-derive the system. The boundary is deliberately fuzzy at GraphRAG — these systems straddle retrieval and knowledge representation — but OKT is treated here strictly as a knowledge-graph-and-summarization artifact, not as a RAG hallucination-mitigation artifact.

## 3. Knowledge-Graph Construction from Text

The dominant unit in this literature is the **(subject, predicate, object) triplet**. REBEL (Relation Extraction By End-to-end Language generation), proposed by Pere-Lluís Huguet Cabot and Roberto Navigli at EMNLP 2021, frames relation extraction as an autoregressive seq2seq task on BART-large, outputs a linearized triplet sequence using `<tripplet>`, `<subj>`, `<obj>` marker tokens, and covers more than 200 relation types drawn from Wikidata ([per OKT fact pool, fact:1823bd93-c170-4068-8600-b15fbb0b829a](<fact:1823bd93-c170-4068-8600-b15fbb0b829a>); [fact:4d7c749c-9019-4fda-8aa9-826d1061c2e2](<fact:4d7c749c-9019-4fda-8aa9-826d1061c2e2>)). The REBEL synthesis stored in the OKT concept graph is densely cited and notes that the model is "not designed for open information extraction where relation types are not pre-defined; its outputs are constrained to the set of relation types seen during pre-training and fine-tuning" ([concept:5a9fe41f-aa2a-4b0f-881c-ecfc72368e1f](<concept:5a9fe41f-aa2a-4b0f-881c-ecfc72368e1f>)) — a direct structural constraint: the KG *is* the asserted typed edge set. A second tradition, the OpenIE family, is characterized in the OKT fact pool's LLM-KG-construction survey as one of two paradigms distinguished by whether the schema is fixed: the survey reviews "schema-based paradigms, which emphasize structure, normalization, and consistency; and schema-free paradigms, which highlight flexibility, adaptability, and open discovery" ([fact:0e30ac79-f15c-484e-a5f1-e6ad06626781](<fact:0e30ac79-f15c-484e-a5f1-e6ad06626781>)). Carta et al. 2023 — "Iterative Zero-Shot LLM Prompting for Knowledge Graph Construction" by Alessandro Giuliani, submitted July 2023 — exemplifies the schema-free pole: it leverages GPT-3.5 with iterative zero-shot prompting, contributes "(i) an innovative strategy for iteratively prompting large language models to extract relevant components of the final graph; (ii) a zero-shot strategy for each prompt, eliminating the need for guiding examples; and (iii) a scalable solution that avoids the need for external resources or human expertise" ([fact:a661a3e6-6ba5-4ae6-9236-587b23a20525](<fact:a661a3e6-6ba5-4ae6-9236-587b23a20525>); [fact:cd1cdb2b-9f85-4861-bc7c-08307bd5f770](<fact:cd1cdb2b-9f85-4861-bc7c-08307bd5f770>)). Comet (Bosselut et al., ACL 2019) extends the same constructor logic to commonsense KGs ([fact:67561be0-c31e-423f-adf6-92a65bb4cf37](<fact:67561be0-c31e-423f-adf6-92a65bb4cf37>); [fact:399f1c55-b62e-4a5f-ab73-af45e49e10ce](<fact:399f1c55-b62e-4a5f-ab73-af45e49e10ce>)).

**The common denominator:** these systems *assert typed relations*; the KG *is* the asserted edge set. **OKT departs** on a single structural axis: it emits facts as atomic text rows, derives edges from shared facts (the `concept_relations` materialized view counts distinct shared facts), and asserts *no predicate*. The contrast is sharp: where REBEL's linearization emits `<tripplet> subject <subj> object <obj> relation`, OKT's extraction emits a free-text fact and lets concept membership and embedding distance determine graph connectivity. The agent hypothesis — not stated by any single source in the pool — is that OKT generalizes the Carta/Giuliani schema-free position one step further: not merely "no guiding examples" but "no relation vocabulary at all." Confirming this would require a head-to-head evaluation of OKT versus REBEL or Carta on a shared extraction task, which the fact pool does not contain.

## 4. Top-Down vs Emergent Ontology — Parallel Scenarios

DBpedia and Wikidata anchor the top-down pole. DBpedia "initially started by extracting facts from the infoboxes of specific types of Wikipedia articles, such as those concerning people, cities, companies, and music bands" ([fact:f2bb6d17-f5eb-46e2-b4db-9801887f1344](<fact:f2bb6d17-f5eb-46e2-b4db-9801887f1344>)) and "uses the words from the infoboxes as relation names" rather than "defined relations with ranges and domains" — a design that extracts a wealth of facts at the cost that "the same relationship may appear under different names (e.g., length, length-in-km, length-km)" ([fact:34ac994e-3a3d-4988-b89f-6437c29b386c](<fact:34ac994e-3a3d-4988-b89f-6437c29b386c>); [fact:07b3ed42-dea7-4485-9506-5615c76244f2](<fact:07b3ed42-dea7-4485-9506-5615c76244f2>)). Wikidata uses an explicit item/property model: "almost 10 million items have statements, and more than 30 million statements were created using more than 900 different properties" with the most frequent property P31 ("instance of") used 5.6 million times and the least frequent P485 used 133 times ([fact:9b426355-00dc-4920-b743-5b952abb86cd](<fact:9b426355-00dc-4920-b743-5b952abb86cd>); [fact:7938759c-0759-45b6-b463-b431a2a8b203](<fact:7938759c-0759-45b6-b463-b431a2a8b203>); [fact:437e04f7-bd06-40f4-b687-6a4d32235f92](<fact:437e04f7-bd06-40f4-b687-6a4d32235f92>)). Wikidata "property pages specify a datatype that defines the type of values the property can have" ([fact:37ee9b65-bbe4-4cc6-a787-17af89663454](<fact:37ee9b65-bbe4-4cc6-a787-17af89663454>)) and supports qualifiers to encode ternary relations that "elude the property-value model" ([fact:a20d3785-d262-4272-a454-a8a072ae493b](<fact:a20d3785-d262-4272-a454-a8a072ae493b>)). Both are top-down in the precise sense that **schema precedes data**: properties and their datatypes are declared before statements are authored.

Cimiano's *Ontology Learning and Population from Text* (Springer, 2006) is the bridge work: it "presents both the theoretical foundations and practical methods for automatically constructing and populating ontologies from textual data" and explicitly "aims to assist ontology engineers by offering guidelines grounded in a comparison of different methods" ([concept:b851fb5f-ef36-43c9-a6c3-1480b38cd011](<concept:b851fb5f-ef36-43c9-a6c3-1480b38cd011>); [fact:0991df65-0cc2-44ed-a590-ebe3bcbffe7d](<fact:0991df65-0cc2-44ed-a590-ebe3bcbffe7d>)). Cimiano learns taxonomic structure from text — but still produces an asserted *is-a* hierarchy. He is the inflection point: a top-down ontologist who concedes that the schema should be *learned*.

**Scenario A (top-down is necessary/superior).** Typed schemas are machine-readable, queryable by type, federated, and interoperable. Wikidata's qualifier machinery demonstrates that a property-value-plus-qualifier model can express even ternary relations cleanly ([fact:9898bf9e-9a15-422e-8ade-7c957fa9d1ee](<fact:9898bf9e-9a15-422e-8ade-7c957fa9d1ee>); [fact:2628bc7d-5c10-42cb-b80c-6976a8063fe7](<fact:2628bc7d-5c10-42cb-b80c-6976a8063fe7>)), and its "no value" / "unknown" distinction prevents the "common neighbor" query errors that arise when absence is conflated with falsity ([fact:1c161e4a-f7b4-4aef-b628-f378445e827b](<fact:1c161e4a-f7b4-4aef-b628-f378445e827b>); [fact:13e436a7-0653-47a1-9015-6bfabc90a819](<fact:13e436a7-0653-47a1-9015-6bfabc90a819>)). OG-RAG (Ontology-Guided RAG) exemplifies the gain: on domain-specific query-answering, "the OG-RAG method achieves the highest correctness scores across Soybean (0.84), Wheat (0.82), and News (0.52) datasets, outperforming the RAG, RAPTOR, and GraphRAG methods" ([fact:e1c17491-3c3f-4083-8ece-fe568978d375](<fact:e1c17491-3c3f-4083-8ece-fe568978d375>)) and provides "easy-to-attribute contexts that require minimal effort from users to trace the generation of the response" ([fact:d749d6bb-0713-431c-862e-36fde16d33ba](<fact:d749d6bb-0713-431c-862e-36fde16d33ba>)). The top-down case rests on queryability and attribution.

**Scenario B (emergent is necessary/superior).** Any predefined schema excludes what does not fit. DBpedia itself demonstrates the cost: "the consistency and accuracy of DBpedia are unknown" ([fact:56c86c85-033e-4582-8180-c8b7d8d70778](<fact:56c86c85-033e-4582-8180-c8b7d8d70778>)) and its free-form relation naming produces synonym duplication ([fact:07b3ed42-dea7-4485-9506-5615c76244f2](<fact:07b3ed42-dea7-4485-9506-5615c76244f2>)). Cimiano concedes by *learning* ontologies from text rather than asserting them a priori. OKT generalizes further: **no schema at all except context vocabulary**. In OKT, the only top-down element is the `context` typing label (a string such as "Concept", "Model", "Person", drawn from a vocabulary OKT seeds from DBpedia L3 labels) — and it is a typing label, not a relational schema. The emergent case rests on coverage and on not foreclosing what the corpus has to say.

**Where they converge:** both extract from text; both can be LLM-driven (Carta et al. demonstrate LLM-driven construction; Cimiano pre-dates LLMs but his learning paradigm is LLM-extendable). **Where they diverge:** asserted structure versus derived structure. **The discriminating experiment** — has anyone shown that a maximally-emergent graph (no predicates, edges by shared-fact count) answers the same class of questions as a typed KG with comparable accuracy? — is **open**. No fact in the pool directly tests it. OKT sits at the maximally-emergent end; its context vocabulary is the only top-down residue, and it is a typing label rather than a relational schema.

## 5. Triplet KGs vs OKT's Fact+Concept Graph — Parallel Scenarios

This is the central structural comparison. A triplet KG has **nodes = entities, edges = typed predicates, single-node-per-entity**. OKT has **concepts (many-per-canonical-name, one per context) + facts + a fact_concepts junction + a concept_relations materialized view that is derived, weighted by `COUNT(DISTINCT fact_id)`**. The two are not minor variants; they are different data models.

**Scenario A (typed relations are right).** Multi-hop reasoning over a structured KB requires typed paths. SubgraphRAG / SG-RAG MOT (Saleh, Tur, Saygin, *Machine Learning and Knowledge Extraction*, 2025) is explicitly a "SubGraph Retrieval Augmented Generation with Merging and Ordering Triplets for Knowledge Graph Multi-Hop Question Answering" system ([fact:895adca5-b30a-4464-b578-8a65a3e4729b](<fact:895adca5-b30a-4464-b578-8a65a3e4729b>)) whose "evaluations on the WebQSP and CWQ benchmarks demonstrate that SubgraphRAG improves efficiency, accuracy, and reliability by reducing hallucinations and improving response grounding" ([fact:7b6963a3-f4a7-46c7-917d-cc682235e3bc](<fact:7b6963a3-f4a7-46c7-917d-cc682235e3bc>); [fact:172127f6-7061-44a7-b1a9-1b4c7e587f9f](<fact:172127f6-7061-44a7-b1a9-1b4c7e587f9f>)). Think-on-Graph (ToG) is "an implementation of the LLM⊗KG paradigm in which the LLM agent iteratively executes beam search on a knowledge graph (KG), discovers the most promising reasoning paths, and returns the most likely reasoning results" ([fact:1bff2bed-0023-4242-a47f-1493dec61ebb](<fact:1bff2bed-0023-4242-a47f-1493dec61ebb>)). Both rely on **typed paths for traversal**; without predicates there is no path, only co-occurrence. On this reading, OKT's matview is a similarity co-occurrence structure, not a reasoning substrate.

**Scenario B (derived relations are right).** Every asserted triple can be wrong, and extraction errors propagate downstream with no fact pool to audit against. REBEL's own synthesis concedes that the model is "susceptible to exposure bias" ([concept:5a9fe41f-aa2a-4b0f-881c-ecfc72368e1f](<concept:5a9fe41f-aa2a-4b0f-881c-ecfc72368e1f>)) and that "a relation's presence in Wikidata does not guarantee it is entailed in the specific text" — REBEL's authors used a RoBERTa NLI filter but acknowledge prior methods were "noisy for frequent relations" ([concept:5a9fe41f-aa2a-4b0f-881c-ecfc72368e1f](<concept:5a9fe41f-aa2a-4b0f-881c-ecfc72368e1f>)). OKT's matview is `COUNT(DISTINCT fact_id)`: it is **always consistent with the fact pool by construction**. The cost is representational — no "X causes Y" is representable as a typed edge; an agent reads shared facts to infer the predicate. The benefit is auditability: every edge traces to fact rows that trace to source URLs.

**When each is appropriate.** Triplet KGs suit typed-path multi-hop reasoning over structured KBs (WebQSP, CWQ, Freebase-style corpora). OKT suits fact-level verification and citation in domains with no pre-built ontology and where the goal is auditability rather than path traversal. **Symmetric skepticism:** OKT can fragment without aggressive pruning (a Scope A concern not re-derived here); the triplet approach can shatter when extraction errors propagate with no fact-pool fallback. Neither dominates; they optimize different objective functions.

## 6. Entity Disambiguation with Embeddings

The entity-linking (EL) tradition is structured as **mention detection → candidate generation → disambiguation**, with disambiguation "generally the last step in an Entity Linking system (Broscheit, 2019), occurring immediately after mention detection and candidate generation" ([fact:b142babf-4a9c-48d0-b883-dc939122f1c2](<fact:b142babf-4a9c-48d0-b883-dc939122f1c2>)). Kolitsas, Ganea, and Hofmann's *End-to-End Neural Entity Linking* (CoNLL 2018) is a neural end-to-end model "trained by enforcing the scores of gold entity-mention pairs to be higher than all possible scores of incorrect candidates or invalid mentions to jointly make entity disambiguation (ED) and mention detection (MD) decisions" ([fact:7c73c73c-f78d-428a-9e00-27022c8c070b](<fact:7c73c73c-f78d-428a-9e00-27022c8c070b>); [fact:85c96123-9e50-4a84-9abb-b22f748c3ea6](<fact:85c96123-9e50-4a84-9abb-b22f748c3ea6>)). GENRE (De Cao et al., 2020) "retrieves entities by generating their unique names, left to right, token-by-token in an autoregressive fashion" ([fact:f5f74c8a-7bf4-4ac9-ae3a-3efa4c46f062](<fact:f5f74c8a-7bf4-4ac9-ae3a-3efa4c46f062>)). BLINK (Wu, Petroni, Josifoski, Riedel, Zettlemoyer, EMNLP 2020) is a "Scalable Zero-shot Entity Linking with Dense Entity Retrieval" system from Facebook AI Research whose bi-encoder (large) has 680M parameters and cross-encoder (large) 340M ([fact:83db1f88-34e4-4095-8f36-c615910c120e](<fact:83db1f88-34e4-4095-8f36-c615910c120e>); [fact:0dcb44fb-7a24-4ff6-8d75-c5df152e29b9](<fact:0dcb44fb-7a24-4ff6-8d75-c5df152e29b9>)) and whose cross-encoder "predicts a more specific entity" than the bi-encoder on the same mention ([fact:998e5217-4765-44e2-b8f4-6bec84e0295e](<fact:998e5217-4765-44e2-b8f4-6bec84e0295e>)). Ganea and Hofmann's *Deep Joint Entity Disambiguation with Local Neural Attention* (EMNLP 2017) is the local-attention predecessor in the same lineage ([fact:e6753214-c5f7-44fb-b8c8-34d55799c415](<fact:e6753214-c5f7-44fb-b8c8-34d55799c415>)).

**Shared structure:** all of these operate **per mention**, against an **external KB**, with a **predefined candidate set**. OKT's `ResolveAliasMatchForFact` is structurally different on all three axes: it operates **per fact** (not per mention), against the **repository's own concept set** (not an external KB), and uses **cosine tie-break** (not cross-encoder re-rank). The strength is granularity: two facts in the same sentence that both use the surface form "N" can route to different concepts because disambiguation is fact-at-a-time. The weakness is scope: there is no cross-document external linking (concepts are repo-internal), disambiguation quality is bounded by the embedding model, and there is no cross-encoder re-rank to recover specificity the way BLINK does ([fact:998e5217-4765-44e2-b8f4-6bec84e0295e](<fact:998e5217-4765-44e2-b8f4-6bec84e0295e>)). The agent hypothesis — not stated by any source — is that OKT trades external-KB grounding for per-fact resolution precision; confirming this would require a benchmark like GERBIL (referenced in the pool as the platform comparing "AIDA, FOX, Babelfy, Entityclassifier.eu, Kea, DBpedia Spotlight, WAT" ([fact:f900d8ad-14aa-46a3-82fc-a32223cf6074](<fact:f900d8ad-14aa-46a3-82fc-a32223cf6074>); [fact:138a888e-1f96-4582-8bcc-37bbadc261d9](<fact:138a888e-1f96-4582-8bcc-37bbadc261d9>))) adapted to fact-level rather than mention-level evaluation.

## 7. Deduplication via Embedding Distance

Barlaug and Gulla's *Neural Networks for Entity Matching: A Survey* (ACM TKDD, April 2021) is the OKT pool's anchor for this body ([fact:6b717391-7738-4e60-972e-bbf86d4de6ae](<fact:6b717391-7738-4e60-972e-bbf86d4de6ae>); [fact:b52d85de-7cb3-4773-8f48-90cc7bb1fbc6](<fact:b52d85de-7cb3-4773-8f48-90cc7bb1fbc6>)). It frames a traditional entity-matching process as five sequential steps — "(1) Data pre-processing of data sources A and B, (2) Schema matching, (3) Blocking, (4) Record pair comparison, and (5) Classification, which results in the final Matches M" ([fact:7371b041-bb02-478d-b1e5-509a540750e1](<fact:7371b041-bb02-478d-b1e5-509a540750e1>)) — and notes that "schema matching and blocking steps in entity matching have traditionally been solved with specialized methods separate from record pair comparison and classification" ([fact:79a8cb6b-21c1-4bfa-b7b8-49f78fcf2b29](<fact:79a8cb6b-21c1-4bfa-b7b8-49f78fcf2b29>)). Critically, "as of the 2021 survey, there is no neural network method that provides an end-to-end solution integrating schema matching, blocking, record pair comparison, and classification" ([fact:c6975aea-6972-43a0-9050-a48cb9f52b66](<fact:c6975aea-6972-43a0-9050-a48cb9f52b66>)) — a gap the authors flag as a target. The survey's Table 2 enumerates which neural methods hit which steps ([fact:dd4ecb7a-fe6a-493e-a7e5-45436c201a39](<fact:dd4ecb7a-fe6a-493e-a7e5-45436c201a39>); [fact:d85cc6b0-4ad0-49db-87af-efa222d279a2](<fact:d85cc6b0-4ad0-49db-87af-efa222d279a2>)), and Table 1 catalogues the synonym cloud around the task ("Entity matching, Entity resolution, Record linkage, Data matching, Data linkage, Reference reconciliation, String matching, ... Deduplication, Duplicate detection, Merge-purge, ...") ([fact:73d4f5d3-fa29-4d9f-abbd-7ee2569e9111](<fact:73d4f5d3-fa29-4d9f-abbd-7ee2569e9111>)) — the latter confirming that OKT's `deduplicate_facts` task is one variant of a long-studied problem.

OKT's `deduplicate_facts` is a **single-linkage** scheme: nearest-neighbor-at-threshold, stable-wins, with a `mergeSources` step that relinks the loser's sources, references, and concepts onto the winner. The provenance-preservation guarantee is that the winner carries the union of non-overlapping citations. **Cost:** a false merge (threshold too low) unions citations from distinct facts, producing a fact row whose source list over-claims. **Benefit:** correct merges accumulate provenance, and the accumulation *compounds* across runs. **Symmetric skepticism:** OKT's tie-break is deterministic but **order-dependent** — and order-dependence is a known weakness flagged by EM surveys in the sense that no surveyed method achieves the end-to-end integration Barlaug and Gulla call for ([fact:c6975aea-6972-43a0-9050-a48cb9f52b66](<fact:c6975aea-6972-43a0-9050-a48cb9f52b66>)). OKT accepts order-dependence deliberately, to catch same-batch duplicates that a symmetric scheme would miss. Whether this trade is net-positive is not tested in the pool.

## 8. GraphRAG and Competitor Systems

The GraphRAG family is the most directly comparable cluster. The OKT pool's GraphRAG anchor is "GraphRAG: Unlocking LLM Discovery on Narrative Private Data," authored by Larson and Truitt (2024) and made available via Microsoft Research ([fact:ca72ea19-2aa2-4e4f-956e-9dbfbd5fa6d1](<fact:ca72ea19-2aa2-4e4f-956e-9dbfbd5fa6d1>)). The fact pool does not contain a detailed fact stating the Microsoft GraphRAG "substantial improvements on comprehensiveness/diversity" claim; the OG-RAG comparison facts, however, report that OG-RAG "outperform[s] the RAG, RAPTOR, and GraphRAG methods" on correctness across Soybean/Wheat/News ([fact:e1c17491-3c3f-4083-8ece-fe568978d375](<fact:e1c17491-3c3f-4083-8ece-fe568978d375>)) — confirming GraphRAG is a live competitor in these benchmarks but is beaten by the ontology-guided variant on domain-specific correctness. LightRAG (Guo, Xia, Yu, Ao, Huang, arXiv:2410.05779, 2024) is "a framework proposed to address the limitations of existing RAG systems by incorporating graph structures into text indexing and retrieval processes" ([fact:c18c6b79-f7e3-4d46-a52d-bd2e6e47f964](<fact:c18c6b79-f7e3-4d46-a52d-bd2e6e47f964>); [fact:c49b7cbf-c791-4f10-a241-af1ceb8c82ab](<fact:c49b7cbf-c791-4f10-a241-af1ceb8c82ab>)), "employs a dual-level retrieval system that enhances comprehensive information retrieval from both low-level and high-level knowledge discovery" ([fact:525b7b1d-c732-448b-9e8b-feebf7627be5](<fact:525b7b1d-c732-448b-9e8b-feebf7627be5>)), and crucially "utilizes an incremental update algorithm that ensures the timely integration of new data, allowing the system to remain effective and responsive in rapidly changing data environments" ([fact:80d6a01b-e6e8-4b79-8d90-670a37fd4b93](<fact:80d6a01b-e6e8-4b79-8d90-670a37fd4b93>)) — making it the closest architectural relative to OKT's compounding behavior. SubgraphRAG / SG-RAG MOT (Saleh et al., 2025) is the triplet-ordering multi-hop system noted above ([fact:895adca5-b30a-4464-b578-8a65a3e4729b](<fact:895adca5-b30a-4464-b578-8a65a3e4729b>); [fact:7b6963a3-f4a7-46c7-917d-cc682235e3bc](<fact:7b6963a3-f4a7-46c7-917d-cc682235e3bc>)). Think-on-Graph (Sun et al., 2023) is the LLM-agent-beam-search system over a typed KG ([fact:1bff2bed-0023-4242-a47f-1493dec61ebb](<fact:1bff2bed-0023-4242-a47f-1493dec61ebb>)). OG-RAG (Sharma et al., 2025) is the opposite-of-OKT on the ontology axis: a top-down ontology-guided hypergraph system whose "authors leverage ontology for general-purpose domain-specific retrieval to bridge a gap in the literature regarding ontology-guided RAG" ([fact:1d61437e-4f09-40ac-b374-37649f7da509](<fact:1d61437e-4f09-40ac-b374-37649f7da509>)) and whose Bran et al. (2025) / Xiao et al. (2024) lineage is explicitly named ([fact:41d5d2da-0c66-47e1-a074-28a856d901f8](<fact:41d5d2da-0c66-47e1-a074-28a856d901f8>)). RAPTOR (Sarthi et al., 2024) — "Recursive Abstractive Processing for Tree-Organized Retrieval" — "recursively embeds, clusters, and summarizes chunks of text to construct a tree with differing levels of summarization from the bottom up" ([fact:38e8cab3-9137-42df-b282-68997f6a1004](<fact:38e8cab3-9137-42df-b282-68997f6a1004>); [fact:c750861e-509a-488c-bbcd-80fd5559369c](<fact:c750861e-509a-488c-bbcd-80fd5559369c>)) and "achieves state-of-the-art results on question-answering tasks that involve complex, multi-step reasoning" ([fact:057412ed-f52e-4971-a103-ba23b632718b](<fact:057412ed-f52e-4971-a103-ba23b632718b>)); it is the most directly comparable hierarchical-summarization-plus-retrieval system. A Pseudo-Knowledge Graph (PKG) framework is also present in the pool, integrating "Meta-path Retrieval, In-graph Text, and Vector Retrieval into Large Language Models (LLMs) to overcome the limitations of RAG regarding comprehensive retrieval and relational awareness" ([fact:55a7c02b-45f8-4582-b355-e9b680e8e1cc](<fact:55a7c02b-45f8-4582-b355-e9b680e8e1cc>)) and storing "entities and their attributes as nodes, and relationships as represented as edges" ([fact:8f5b53b3-e841-403c-897d-1ec269e85503](<fact:8f5b53b3-e841-403c-897d-1ec269e85503>)) — a hybrid that, unlike OKT, retains explicit edges.

**Parallel scenarios — community summaries vs per-concept syntheses.** *Scenario A (GraphRAG-style community summaries):* the unit is the *community*; the LLM builds an entity graph, pregenerates community summaries, and runs map-reduce over communities for global questions. The output is ephemeral — rebuilt per corpus — and optimized for global sensemaking across entities. *Scenario B (OKT per-concept syntheses):* the unit is the *concept group*; summary slices (BatchSize=20 facts, one LLM call per slice, batch-only mode) feed a per-canonical-name group synthesis that is **persistent**, **compounds** across runs (no-delta skip), and is returned by `getConcept` as an **authoritative citable definition** with `[text](<fact:fact_id>)` links. **Neither is "better."** GraphRAG is optimized for global cross-entity questions ("what are the main themes across this corpus?"); OKT is optimized for per-concept questions ("what is *this* concept, according to the fact pool?") plus fact-level verification. LightRAG's incremental update ([fact:80d6a01b-e6e8-4b79-8d90-670a37fd4b93](<fact:80d6a01b-e6e8-4b79-8d90-670a37fd4b93>)) is the closest architectural relative to OKT's compounding behavior, but LightRAG's output is still a transient answer, not a persistent citable definition. SubgraphRAG and ToG require a **pre-existing typed KG** — there is no OKT analogue, because OKT has no typed predicates. OG-RAG is **opposite** on the ontology axis: top-down ontology-guided, where OKT is maximally emergent; the OG-RAG correctness gains ([fact:e1c17491-3c3f-4083-8ece-fe568978d375](<fact:e1c17491-3c3f-4083-8ece-fe568978d375>)) are the strongest single piece of evidence in the pool *for* the top-down pole, and OKT has no comparable benchmark result in the pool to set against it.

## 9. Hierarchical Summarization

Christensen et al. 2014 (*Hierarchical Summarization*) frames multi-document summarization as a hierarchy in which subtopics are summarized independently and then combined; the OKT fact pool does not contain a direct Christensen fact, so the claim is referenced here only via the prompt's framing — flagged explicitly as **not located in the pool** (see §12). HiBERT (Zhang et al., ACL 2019) is present: "HIBERT: Document Level Pre-training of Hierarchical Bidirectional Transformers for Document Summarization" applies "the hierarchical bidirectional transformer encoder HIBERT to a document D to yield context dependent representations for all sentences" ([fact:fb7b2eae-dde1-45a6-b6b3-46e23a759001](<fact:fb7b2eae-dde1-45a6-b6b3-46e23a759001>); [fact:134d452e-83ba-4f03-97ca-28107bc6993b](<fact:134d452e-83ba-4f03-97ca-28107bc6993b>); [fact:d2761b68-d957-45c3-8685-a2baf260f9bf](<fact:d2761b68-d957-45c3-8685-a2baf260f9bf>)). Its architecture is "a 'Sent Encoder Transformer' processing sentence-level embeddings ... and a 'Doc Encoder Transformer' that processes the outputs of the sentence encoder" ([fact:84eef0df-7ffc-4e87-861b-d4fdfc26f34b](<fact:84eef0df-7ffc-4e87-861b-d4fdfc26f34b>); [fact:425d69b7-20b6-44d3-b39d-2b97e825b39c](<fact:425d69b7-20b6-44d3-b39d-2b97e825b39c>)), and "the HIBERTS model outperforms BERT by 0.4 to 0.5 ROUGE while using only half the number of model parameters" ([fact:e8d4cda8-8a75-4308-ae65-9b5cbd45df67](<fact:e8d4cda8-8a75-4308-ae65-9b5cbd45df67>)) — on CNN/DM, HIBERTM reaches R-1 42.37 / R-2 19.38 / R-L 38.83 ([fact:614fba0f-1841-49d9-b554-d6e8beedeb6f](<fact:614fba0f-1841-49d9-b554-d6e8beedeb6f>)).

OKT's two-tier hierarchy **rhymes** with HiBERT's sentence→document structure and with map-reduce, but the **units are facts, not sentences or documents**. The first tier is summary slices: BatchSize=20 facts, one LLM call per slice, `[text](<fact:fact_id>)` citations, batch-only mode capping cost. The second tier is group synthesis: one per canonical-name group, no-delta skip, graph-aware related-concepts block, image picker, attribution-grounded prompt. **Comparison:** the slice→synthesis pattern rhymes with HiBERT's sent-encoder→doc-encoder, but OKT's hierarchy is **LLM-call orchestration over a fact pool**, not a **model architecture**. HiBERT is a pre-trained transformer for extractive document summarization; OKT is a pipeline that calls an external LLM per slice and per group. The output is also different: HiBERT emits an extractive summary; OKT emits a **persistent citable definition** with fact-level links, returned by `getConcept` as the authoritative synthesis. RAPTOR ([fact:38e8cab3-9137-42df-b282-68997f6a1004](<fact:38e8cab3-9137-42df-b282-68997f6a1004>)) is closer in spirit — recursive embed/cluster/summarize — but RAPTOR's tree is rebuilt per corpus and used at inference for retrieval, whereas OKT's syntheses are persisted and compound. The agent hypothesis, not stated by any source: OKT's hierarchy is best read as **RAPTOR over fact atoms rather than document chunks**, with the additional property that the top tier is a *named, queryable, citable definition* rather than a retrieval index entry. Confirming this would require adapting RAPTOR's collapsed-tree retrieval evaluation to a fact-pool setting.

## 10. Where OKT Sits Relative to the Literature

- **Versus triplet KGs:** facts not triplets; derived not asserted; always consistent with the fact pool by construction (`COUNT(DISTINCT fact_id)`); no typed predicate representable as an edge; agent reads shared facts to infer predicates.
- **Versus top-down ontology:** maximally emergent; the only top-down element is context vocabulary (a typing label), and it is not a relational schema. Cimiano learns ontologies; OKT drops the ontology and keeps the concept graph.
- **Versus entity linking:** per-fact not per-mention; against the repo's own concept set not an external KB; cosine tie-break not cross-encoder re-rank. Finer granularity (per-fact), narrower scope (no external grounding).
- **Versus EM/dedup:** single-linkage, relink-loser-sources pattern preserves provenance; order-dependent tie-break accepted as a deliberate trade to catch same-batch duplicates. Barlaug and Gulla's 2021 end-to-end gap ([fact:c6975aea-6972-43a0-9050-a48cb9f52b66](<fact:c6975aea-6972-43a0-9050-a48cb9f52b66>)) remains open; OKT does not close it.
- **Versus GraphRAG/LightRAG/SubgraphRAG/ToG/OG-RAG:** emergent not LLM-built-per-session (contrast GraphRAG); persistent not ephemeral (contrast GraphRAG community summaries); fact-level not community-summary-level (contrast GraphRAG); no typed paths (contrast SubgraphRAG, ToG); compounding like LightRAG's incremental update ([fact:80d6a01b-e6e8-4b79-8d90-670a37fd4b93](<fact:80d6a01b-e6e8-4b79-8d90-670a37fd4b93>)) but output is a definition not an answer; opposite OG-RAG on the ontology axis, with OG-RAG holding the strongest top-down benchmark result in the pool ([fact:e1c17491-3c3f-4083-8ece-fe568978d375](<fact:e1c17491-3c3f-4083-8ece-fe568978d375>)).
- **Versus HiBERT/Christensen/RAPTOR:** slice→synthesis rhymes with map-reduce, but units are facts not documents/sentences; output is a persistent citable definition not a transient answer or extractive summary; hierarchy is LLM-call orchestration, not a pre-trained encoder.

## 11. What OKT Does NOT Do (Honest Gaps)

These are **scope boundaries, not failures.** OKT does not: (a) emit typed predicates — there is no `(s,p,o)` with relation types, so "X causes Y" is not representable as a typed edge; (b) perform cross-document entity linking with external KBs — concepts are repo-internal, so OKT cannot ground a mention to Wikidata/DBpedia the way BLINK or DBpedia Spotlight do; (c) produce community-level global summaries — only per-concept syntheses, so global cross-entity sensemaking is out of scope; (d) support multi-hop path reasoning over typed edges — the matview is a weighted co-occurrence structure, not a path substrate, so SubgraphRAG/ToG-style traversal is not available; (e) use cross-encoder re-ranking in disambiguation — only cosine tie-break, so the specificity recovery BLINK's cross-encoder provides ([fact:998e5217-4765-44e2-b8f4-6bec84e0295e](<fact:998e5217-4765-44e2-b8f4-6bec84e0295e>)) is absent; (f) use a pre-trained hierarchical encoder — OKT's hierarchy is orchestration over an external LLM, not a HiBERT-style model. **OKT's contribution is the combination:** fact atoms + emergent concept graph + per-fact disambiguation + provenance-preserving dedup + persistent compounding syntheses with fact-level citations. No single predecessor in the pool combines all five. REBEL has triplet atoms but no emergent graph; GraphRAG has an emergent graph but no persistent fact pool; BLINK has disambiguation but no dedup-with-provenance; HiBERT has hierarchy but over documents not facts and without citation links; RAPTOR has recursive summarization but ephemeral retrieval-target output; OG-RAG has ontology-guided attribution but top-down not emergent. The novelty is the assembly, not any single component.
